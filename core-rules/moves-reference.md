## QUICK DECISION TREE

**When a player describes an action:**

```
Is it within their capability? → NO → They can't do it (fiction says no)
                               ↓
                              YES
                               ↓
Does anyone oppose it? → YES → ROLL DICE (contested)
                        ↓
                       NO
                        ↓
Would failure be interesting? → YES → ROLL DICE (vs. difficulty)
                               ↓
                              NO
                               ↓
                    IT JUST HAPPENS (say yes)
```

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE: SAY YES OR ROLL THE DICE

**Default to "yes"** - If a player describes their character doing something and:
- It's within their capability
- No one objects
- Failure wouldn't be interesting

**Then it just happens.** No roll needed.

**Roll dice when:**
- Success is uncertain
- Failure would be interesting
- Someone opposes the action
- Stakes matter to the story

**Every roll answers: "Can I change the fiction in this way?"**

### Declaring Actions (Not Asking Permission)

Don't ask "Can I...?" - Instead, **embed the truth** in your declaration:

| Instead of asking... | Embed the truth... |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| "Can I hack the door?" | "**As I hack the door**, I notice the security is outdated..." |
| "Do I know someone here?" | "**When I scan the crowd**, I recognize my old contact..." |
| "Is there a way around?" | "**While I plot the route**, I know there's a path through the storms..." |

**The structure**: Action + Embedded truth + Opening for complication

This creates collaborative storytelling where you're declaring what happens and the GM responds with complications, opportunities, or calls for rolls.

## SETTING STAKES (ALWAYS BEFORE ROLLING)

Before dice touch the table, everyone agrees on:
- **What happens if you succeed?**
- **What happens if you fail?**
- **What happens on a tie?**

These stakes are **binding**. When dice settle, the outcome happens immediately. No negotiation after the fact.

### Stakes Examples

**Simple hack**: "I'm cracking their security"
- Success: You're in, clean and undetected
- Failure: Alarm triggers, they trace your location
- Tie: You're in but they know someone breached them

**Social pressure**: "I'm convincing them to help us"
- Success: They commit resources and support
- Failure: They refuse and alert their superiors
- Tie: They'll help but want something in return

**Chase sequence**: "We're racing to the extraction point"
- Success: You escape clean
- Failure: Captured or forced to abandon the mission
- Tie: You escape but lose something valuable

## ROLLING DICE

Roll **4 Fate dice** (each shows +, 0, or -) plus your relevant **Stat** (+0 to +3).

Compare to:
- **Difficulty** (GM sets) for unopposed actions
- **Opposing roll** for contested actions

### Difficulties

| Difficulty | Description | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| +0 | Routine | Only roll if there's pressure or opposition |
| +2 | Challenging | Professional skill needed |
| +4 | Expert | Requires specialized training |
| +6 | Near-impossible | Masters struggle with this |
| +8 | Miraculous | Shouldn't be possible |

### Success Levels

| Result | Outcome | What Happens |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Fail by 3+** | Disaster | Serious consequences, major complications |
| **Fail by 1-2** | Failure | Don't get what you want, minor complications |
| **Tie (0)** | Success at cost | Get what you want but GM offers hard choice |
| **Succeed by 1-2** | Success | You get what you want |
| **Succeed by 3+** | Success with style | Extra benefit or gain a Fate point |

**Shifts** = The difference between your roll and difficulty/opposition. Used to determine stress dealt in conflicts.

## BASIC MOVES

### Act Under Pressure
**When you attempt something risky or difficult**, roll the relevant stat.
- **Success**: You do it
- **Failure**: You don't, or you succeed at a cost (take stress, complication, lose something)

**Which stat?** Choose based on how you're doing it:
- Dodge gunfire → Reflexes
- Hold ground when threatened → Cool
- Endure toxins → Body
- Crack a lock quickly → Tech
- Hack under ICE assault → Code

**Example**: *"I dive behind cover as the enforcer opens fire."* Roll Reflexes vs. difficulty +2. Success means you're safe. Failure means you take stress or expose someone else.

---

### Read a Person or Situation
**When you study a person or assess a situation**, roll Cool (people) or Code (systems).
- **Success**: Ask the GM **one question** from the list below
- **Success with style**: Ask **two questions**

**Questions you can ask:**
- What are they really feeling?
- What do they want most?
- What should I be worried about?
- How could I get them to ___?
- What's the weakness here?
- Who's really in control?

**GM answers honestly.** This is information gathering, not mind reading.

**Example**: *"I watch the corporate exec during negotiations."* Roll Cool +2 vs. difficulty +2. Success: "What should I be worried about?" GM: "She keeps glancing at the door. She's expecting someone."

---

### Help or Interfere
**When you help or hinder another character**, describe how and roll the relevant stat.
- **Success helping**: They get **+1** to their roll
- **Success hindering**: They get **-2** to their roll
- **Failure**: You're exposed to the same consequences they face

**You must be in position to help/hinder** - the fiction must support it.

**Example**: *"While Karo hacks the terminal, I monitor security feeds and warn them when guards approach."* Roll Code +2 to help. Success: Karo gets +1 to their hacking roll.

---

### Create Advantage
**When you set up a beneficial situation**, roll the relevant stat.
- **Success**: Create a **scene aspect** with one free invoke
- **Success with style**: Create aspect with **two free invokes**
- **Failure**: Don't create the aspect, or you create it but the enemy gets the free invoke

**Scene aspects** last until the scene ends or they're no longer true. Anyone can invoke them (if it makes sense).

**Examples:**
- *"I set up a sniper position with clean sightlines"* (Reflexes) → Create **High Ground**
- *"I plant evidence implicating the rival corp"* (Cool) → Create **False Lead**
- *"I backdoor their security system"* (Code) → Create **Compromised Network**

**Free invokes** can be used without spending a Fate point - just once, then they're gone.

## EXTENDED CONTESTS

**When one roll isn't enough** - use extended contests for:
- Chases and pursuits
- Prolonged combat
- Hacking through layered ICE
- Extended social pressure
- Anything where progress matters

### How It Works

1. **Set stakes**: What does victory/defeat mean?
2. **Set victory condition**: First to 3 successes, first to fill stress track, extract data before lockdown
3. **Roll each exchange**: Winner deals shifts as stress to loser, or scores a success
4. **Track progress**: Mark successes or stress boxes
5. **Resolve when condition met**: Stakes happen completely

### Example: Hacking Run

**Stakes**: Get the data (win) vs. Black ICE fries your interface (lose)
**Victory condition**: First to fill 3 stress boxes loses

**Exchange 1**: You roll Code +3 = +5, ICE rolls +3. You win by 2 shifts. ICE marks 2 Systems stress.
**Exchange 2**: You roll +2, ICE rolls +4. ICE wins by 2 shifts. You mark 2 Systems stress.
**Exchange 3**: You roll +6 (and invoke **Ex-Corporate Netrunner** for style), ICE rolls +3. You win by 3 shifts. ICE's Systems stress fills. You get the data clean.

## FATE POINT ECONOMY

### Starting Amount
- **3 Fate points** at the start of each session (your Refresh)
- Carry over between scenes but never start a session with less than 3

### Spend Fate Points To:

| Cost | Effect |
|------|--------|
| 1 FP | **Invoke an aspect** for +2 to your roll (after rolling) |
| 1 FP | **Invoke an aspect** to reroll all four dice |
| 1 FP | **Refuse a compel** (keep control of your character) |
| 1 FP | **Declare a story detail** (GM has final approval) |
| 1 FP | **Power certain talents** (if talent specifies) |

### Gain Fate Points When:

| Gain | How |
|------|-----|
| +1 FP | **GM compels your aspect** (accept complication) |
| +1 FP | **Succeed with style** and choose FP instead of benefit |
| +1 FP | **Concede a conflict** before being taken out |
| +1 FP per consequence | Concede after taking consequences in that conflict |

### Invoking Aspects

**You must explain how the aspect helps** in this specific situation.

**Examples:**
- High Concept **Ex-Corporate Security Auditor** → +2 when infiltrating corporate facility (*"I audited this building, I know their protocols"*)
- Connection **My Sister Works Sidereal** → Reroll when negotiating with station security (*"I mention my sister's name, they know her"*)
- Trouble **Can't Say No to Refugees** → +2 when helping desperate people (*"This is what I do, I help people like this"*)

**You can invoke your Trouble** when it helps you, not just when it complicates things.

**Free invokes** from Create Advantage don't cost FP - use them before they expire.

### Compelling Aspects

**When an aspect complicates your situation**, GM offers a Fate point if you accept the complication.

**Example**: *"Your Trouble is 'I Follow Orders, Even Bad Ones.' Your employer orders you to do something you know is wrong. Take a Fate point if you do it anyway?"*

**You can:**
- **Accept**: Take the FP and the complication
- **Refuse**: Spend 1 FP to avoid the complication

**Players can suggest compels** on their own or others' aspects.

## STRESS & CONSEQUENCES

### Stress Tracks

You have three stress tracks (base 3 boxes each, modified by chrome):

| Track | Represents | Examples |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Meat** | Physical harm | Bullets, falls, toxins, exhaustion |
| **Nerves** | Psychological strain | Fear, pressure, betrayal, stress |
| **Systems** | Chrome/gear damage | EMP, hacking, overload, malfunction |

**When you take stress**, mark boxes equal to the shifts:
- Take 3 shifts → mark 3 boxes
- Or mark fewer boxes and take a consequence to absorb the rest

### Consequences

**When you can't or won't mark enough stress**, take a consequence:

| Severity | Absorbs | Clears | Examples |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|
| **Mild** | 2 shifts | After one scene | *Bruised Ribs, Rattled, HUD Glitching* |
| **Moderate** | 4 shifts | Someone helps you recover | *Compound Fracture, PTSD Flashback, Chrome Overheated* |
| **Severe** | 6 shifts | Bought off with advancement | *Lost an Eye, Can't Trust Anyone, Neural Interface Damaged* |

You have **one of each severity** per stress track (3 mild, 3 moderate, 3 severe total).

**Name the consequence** to match the track and how it happened.

**Consequences are aspects** - they're true in the fiction, can be invoked (against you!), and can be compelled.

### Clearing Stress & Consequences

| Type | Clears When |
|------|-------------|
| **Stress boxes** | After the scene ends (catch your breath) |
| **Mild consequence** | After one scene passes |
| **Moderate consequence** | Someone succeeds on appropriate roll to help you (medic, engineer, therapist) |
| **Severe consequence** | Extended narrative work OR bought off during advancement |

**Moderate consequences require active help:**
- Someone with appropriate skill rolls to patch you up
- Success: Consequence clears
- Failure: It stays until next attempt

### Being Taken Out

**If you can't absorb stress** (track full, no consequences available/acceptable), **you're taken out**.

**Whoever took you out decides what happens** (within reason):
- Captured
- Killed
- Humiliated
- Forced to flee
- Chrome destroyed

**This is why consequences exist** - to stay in the fight and maintain some control.

### Conceding

**Before you're taken out**, you can concede the conflict.

**Benefits:**
- Gain 1 FP + 1 FP per consequence taken in this conflict
- You narrate how you lose on your own terms
- You get out, though you don't get what you wanted

**Conceding is losing with dignity** - you don't win, but you control how you lose.

## COMBAT QUICK REFERENCE

### Quick Resolution (Single Roll)

Use when violence is sudden or consequences are clear:

1. **Attacker rolls** Reflexes or Body vs. difficulty or defender's roll
2. **Defender rolls** Reflexes or Body to defend
3. **Shifts** = Meat stress dealt to defender
4. **Defender absorbs** with stress boxes and/or consequences

**Example**: Enforcer attacks with shock baton (Body +3 = +5). You dodge (Reflexes +2 = +3). That's 2 shifts = 2 Meat stress to you.

### Extended Combat (When It Matters)

Use **Overcome Opposition** rules as extended contest:

1. **Set stakes**: What does winning/losing look like?
2. **Each exchange**: Attack and defend rolls
3. **Winner deals shifts** as stress
4. **First to fill stress or concede** loses
5. **Describe each exchange** in the fiction

**This makes combat dramatic** - each exchange is described, stress accumulates, someone concedes or gets taken out.

### Weapons and Armor

**Weapons** don't modify damage - they modify **fictional permission** and **positioning**:
- A knife lets you threaten someone up close
- A rifle lets you attack from across the plaza
- Explosives affect everyone in the area

**Armor** works the same way:
- Light armor might give +1 to defend
- Heavy armor might absorb 1 shift automatically but make you slow
- Some weapons ignore certain armor

**GM determines mechanical effects based on fiction.**

## CHROME AND SYSTEMS DAMAGE

### When Chrome Breaks

Chrome breaks when:
- Systems stress track fills completely
- You take a Systems consequence describing chrome failure
- Specific circumstances trigger (listed in chrome description)

**When chrome breaks:**
- It stops working until repaired
- Check playbook for specific failure effects
- Usually requires Tech roll + time + credits to fix
- Some chrome has catastrophic failure modes

### Maintenance

All chrome requires monthly maintenance (cost listed on playbook).

**If you don't pay maintenance:**
- GM can compel chrome failure at dramatic moments
- Chrome might break on its own
- Eventually permanent degradation

**Maintenance represents:** Cleaning, calibration, software updates, replacement parts, medical checkups.

## COMMON QUESTIONS

### When do I roll?

**Only when**: Success is uncertain, failure is interesting, or someone opposes you. Otherwise, say yes.

### Which stat do I use?

**The one that makes sense for how you're doing it.** Describe your action, then choose the stat.

### Can I retry if I fail?

**Only if the situation changes meaningfully.** The fiction must be different - new approach, new tools, new help, new complication.

### Do I have to take consequences?

**No.** If you'd rather be taken out than suffer the consequence, that's your choice. Sometimes it's better to concede.

### Can I invoke multiple aspects on one roll?

**Yes**, if you have the Fate points and each aspect makes narrative sense.

### What if we disagree on stakes?

**Negotiate before rolling.** If you can't agree, the GM makes the final call (but should be fair and interesting).

### Can the GM compel my aspects in combat?

**Yes.** *"Your Trouble is 'Protect the Innocent.' That kid just ran into the firefight. Take a FP if you break cover to save them?"*

### How many aspects can I have?

**Three permanent**: High Concept, Trouble, Connection. Plus temporary scene aspects from Create Advantage, and consequences (which are aspects).

### What's the difference between a consequence and an aspect?

**Consequences are aspects** - they're just negative ones that came from taking stress. They work exactly like other aspects mechanically.

## EXAMPLES IN PLAY

### Example 1: Simple Success
**Player**: "I hack the door lock."
**GM**: "Corporate security, difficulty +2."
**Player**: *Rolls 4dF+3 (Code) = +4. Success!*
**GM**: "The lock disengages. You're in."

### Example 2: Invoking an Aspect
**Player**: "I hack the door lock."
**GM**: "Difficulty +2."
**Player**: *Rolls 4dF+3 = +1. Failure.*
**Player**: "I'm invoking **Ex-Corporate Security Auditor**. I audited this building - I know their protocols." *Spends 1 FP for +2*
**Result**: +1 becomes +3, success.
**GM**: "You remember the default override they never changed. You're in."

### Example 3: Accepting a Compel
**GM**: "Your Trouble is **Can't Say No to Refugees**. A desperate family just showed up, hunted by Meridian Holdings. Taking them endangers your crew. Accept a FP to help them anyway?"
**Player**: *Takes FP* "Yeah, we're taking them aboard."
**GM**: "Three days later, security finds you. They're wanted for industrial espionage."

### Example 4: Combat Exchange
**GM**: "The enforcer swings a shock baton at you."
**Enforcer**: *Rolls Body +3 = +5*
**Player**: "I dodge!" *Rolls Reflexes +2 = +3*
**GM**: "That's 2 shifts. Take 2 Meat stress or a consequence."
**Player**: *Marks 2 Meat boxes* "I dive behind cover, shoulder numb where it grazed me."

### Example 5: Extended Contest
**GM**: "You're jacked in. Extended contest - Code vs. ICE. First to 4 stress loses. Stakes: Win = get data, Lose = Black ICE fries your interface."
**Exchange 1**: Player rolls Code +4 = +5, ICE rolls +3. Player wins by 2.
**GM**: *Marks 2 ICE stress* "You're breaking through the first layer. It adapts, throws up new barriers..."
**Exchange 2**: Continue until someone hits 4 stress.

## FINAL PRINCIPLE: TO DO IT, DO IT

If you're describing your character doing something, and it makes sense they could, and no one's stopping them, and failure isn't interesting—**just do it**.

The dice come out when:
1. Someone opposes you
2. Failure creates interesting complications
3. Success is genuinely uncertain

**Everything else just happens.**

The game is the conversation. Mechanics resolve uncertainty and distribute narrative authority. Use them when needed. Otherwise, keep talking and keep the story moving.
