# Example: Corporate Infiltrator from Shodann

**Sable Harker** (real name unknown), ex-security auditor with multiple identities

**Stats**: Body +1, Reflexes +2, Code +3, Tech +2, Cool +1

**Stress**: Meat 2 (Neural Deck Port -1), Nerves 3, Systems 6 (Neural Deck Port +2, Pattern Recognition Suite +1)

**Talents**:
- *Backdoor Specialist*: Once per session, automatically succeed at infiltrating a system you've previously audited. You left yourself a way in.
- *Corporate Camouflage* (Passive): +2 to Cool when maintaining cover in corporate environments
- *Information Broker*: Once per session, declare you know someone who has information you need

**Chrome**:
- *Neural Deck Port*: Direct brain-to-system interface. +1 to Code when directly connected.
- *Pattern Recognition Suite*: Enhanced visual processing. +2 to Code when analyzing systems for vulnerabilities.

**Aspects**:
- High Concept: *Ex-AlgoCratic Security Auditor with Backdoors Everywhere*
- Trouble: *Too Many Identities, Forgot Which One Is Real*
- Connection: *This Encrypted File Could Destroy Three Executives*

## Scene: Data Extraction from Golgotha Mining Operations

**The Setup**: Sable needs to extract financial records from Meridian Holdings' offices in Golgotha's corporate tower—thirty floors above the mining operations, pressure-sealed against the deep water outside. The data proves they're falsifying safety inspections. Her contact in the miners' union is counting on her. She's going in as "Ann Sho, network contractor from Sidereal," using credentials she created when she audited Meridian six months ago.

**GM**: "Golgotha tower security is professional. You're in the lobby—corporate reception on your left, security checkpoint ahead. The guard is checking IDs against work orders. You can hear the structural groan of the tower settling against ocean pressure."

**Sable's player**: "I've been here before. I audited their network infrastructure last year under contract. I know the layout."

**GM**: "True. You know the offices are on Floor 28. Security checkpoint first."

**Sable's player**: "I walk up confidently. My credentials say Ann Sho, network contractor, scheduled for maintenance. I've done this before—I look busy, slightly annoyed, like someone called in on their day off."

**GM**: "The guard scans your ID. Cool roll, difficulty +2. He's thorough."

**Roll**: [+1, 0, -1, +1] = +1, +1 Cool = +2 total vs. +2. **Tie.**

**GM**: "He pauses. Looks at his screen. 'Your work order expired two hours ago. Did someone call you back?' He's suspicious but not alarmed yet."

**Sable's player**: "I invoke my *Corporate Camouflage* talent—I've got +2 to Cool maintaining cover. And I sigh, very tired. 'Server's throwing errors again. Your IT lead, Marcus—he called me directly. You want me to leave and let your whole financial system crash overnight?'"

**GM**: "That's convincing. Roll again with your bonus."

**Roll**: [0, 0, +1, 0] = +1, +1 Cool +2 Corporate Camouflage = +4 total vs. +2. **Success by 2.**

**GM**: "He waves you through. 'Twenty-eighth floor. Sign out before you leave.' You're in."

---

**Inside the data center**:

**GM**: "You're alone in the server room. Temperature-controlled, low lighting, the hum of machines. The financial records are on an isolated system—air-gapped. You need physical access. That's a Code roll to crack their security and copy the data. Difficulty +4, military-grade encryption."

**Sable's player**: "I jack in directly with my Neural Deck Port. That's +1 to Code when connected. I'm also using my *Backdoor Specialist* talent—I audited this system six months ago. I left myself a way in. I automatically succeed."

**GM**: "Right. You remember the backdoor—a maintenance account you created and never closed. You're in without a trace. But here's the complication: the files you need have been moved. They're no longer in the expected directory. Someone reorganized the system since you were here."

**Sable's player**: "That's bad. Where are they now?"

**GM**: "You'll need to search for them. That's Code, difficulty +3, and it'll take time. Every minute you're here increases the chance someone notices."

**Roll**: [+1, +1, 0, -1] = +1, +3 Code +1 Neural Deck Port = +5 vs. +3. **Success by 2.**

**GM**: "Your Pattern Recognition Suite kicks in. You see the data flow patterns—the files were archived to a secondary server, probably automated. You locate them. Downloading now. But your deck is heating up. Mark 1 Systems stress as the neural interface strains."

**Sable's player**: *Marks one Systems stress box, 5 remaining.* "How long until download completes?"

**GM**: "Thirty seconds. But you hear the door access beep. Someone's coming in."

---

**Extended Contest: Escape Without Being Identified**

**GM**: "A Meridian security tech walks in—young, professional, not expecting anyone. He sees you jacked into the terminal. This is an extended contest. First to two successes wins. If you win, you get out clean. If he wins, he identifies you before you escape."

**Exchange 1 - Cool vs. his Cool +2**:

**Sable's player**: "I don't disconnect—download isn't done. I look up, confused. 'Hey, you're the on-call tech? Thank god. This server's throwing critical errors. Marcus said you'd meet me here.'"

**Roll**: [-1, 0, +1, 0] = 0, +1 Cool +2 Corporate Camouflage = +3 total vs. his roll.

**GM rolls**: [+1, -1, 0, +1] = +1, +2 Cool = +3. **Tie.**

**GM**: "He's uncertain. 'I'm not on-call tonight. Who authorized you?' He's moving toward his own terminal to check. You both buy time. No success for either side yet."

**Exchange 2 - Code vs. his Tech +2**:

**Sable's player**: "The download finishes. I need to cover my tracks and disconnect. I'm using my Pattern Recognition Suite to erase my access logs in real-time as I pull out. That's Code with +2 from the chrome."

**Roll**: [+1, +1, +1, 0] = +3, +3 Code +1 Neural Deck Port +2 Pattern Recognition = +9 vs. his +2. **Success by 7 shifts—two successes!**

**GM**: "You ghost the logs completely. By the time he pulls up the access records, there's no trace you were ever here. You disconnect, stand up, and shake your head. 'False alarm—looks like it auto-resolved. Sorry to waste your time.' You're already moving toward the door."

**Sable's player**: "I invoke my *Ex-AlgoCratic Auditor* aspect. I've talked my way out of a hundred server rooms. Spending 1 FP for +2 to my Cool as I leave."

**GM**: "He's confused but you're clearly leaving. 'Wait, I need your ID for the report—'"

**Sable's player**: "Over my shoulder: 'Ann Sho, Sidereal contract. Marcus has my details.' And I'm gone before he can react."

**GM**: "You're out. Elevator down. You pass the security guard—he nods. You're clear of the building. The encrypted data is in your concealed storage. The miners' union will have their proof."

---

**Result**: Sable succeeded completely. She extracted the financial records proving Meridian's safety violations. She spent 1 FP on her aspect invoke and took 1 Systems stress from the neural interface strain. Her cover identity "Ann Sho" might be flagged later when the tech files his report and finds no record of Marcus calling anyone—but by then she'll be using a different name. The data is what matters.

## What This Scene Demonstrates

**Code rolls**: Direct system access, backdoor exploitation, pattern recognition

**Cool rolls**: Social infiltration, maintaining cover under pressure, fast-talking

**Extended contest**: Escalating stakes with clear victory condition

**Aspect invocation**: Using High Concept to enhance an action at critical moment

**Talent usage**: Backdoor Specialist for automatic success, Corporate Camouflage for passive bonus

**Chrome mechanics**: Neural Deck Port providing Code bonus, Systems stress from extended use

**Stakes setting**: Clear win/loss conditions established before contest began

**Pelagic setting**: Golgotha tower, pressure groans, corporate offices above deep-water mining

**Infiltrator identity**: Multiple names (Sable Harker operating as Ann Sho), corporate access, technical and social skills combined

**Consequence of success**: Evidence secured but potential future complications (burned cover identity)
