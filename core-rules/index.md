## WHAT IS THIS?

These are the universal game mechanics for **High Tech, Low Lives** - a Fate-based cyberpunk RPG set in a world 93% covered by ocean. Whether you're playing solo, running a crew, or GMing a campaign, these rules form the foundation.

**Core Principle**: The fiction comes first. Mechanics serve story. Dice resolve uncertainty. Stakes matter.

### Building Characters
- Use **8 Build Points** for talents and specialization
- Choose **3 Aspects**: High Concept, Trouble, Connection
- Assign **Stats** (+3, +2, +1, +1, +0): Body, Reflexes, Code, Tech, Cool
- Track **3 Stress Tracks**: Meat, Nerves, Systems
- Start with **3 Fate Points** per session

Full details in [Playing the Fiction](/core-rules/playing-the-fiction.md#character-positioning-build-points)

### Running Games
- Default to "Say Yes or Roll the Dice"
- Set stakes before rolling
- Honor the outcome without hedging
- Let aspects matter in the fiction
- Make every roll count

Philosophy explained in [Playing the Fiction](/core-rules/playing-the-fiction.md#say-yes-or-roll-the-dice)

## THE SYSTEM AT A GLANCE

**Rolling Dice**
- 4 Fate dice (showing +, -, or blank) + relevant Stat
- Compare to difficulty (+0 routine, +2 challenging, +4 expert, +6 near-impossible)
- Or oppose another character's roll

**Aspects**
- True statements about characters, situations, or the world
- Spend 1 Fate Point to invoke for +2 or reroll
- Gain 1 Fate Point when aspects complicate your life
- Make narrative sense when used

**Stress & Consequences**
- Mark stress boxes when you take hits
- When track is full, take a consequence or get taken out
- Consequences persist: Minor (scene), Serious (requires attention), Severe (permanent)
- Three tracks: Meat (physical), Nerves (psychological), Systems (technical)

**Talents**
- Special abilities that break rules in specific ways
- Passive effects (always on) or active (cost Fate Points)
- Purchased with Build Points during character creation
- Examples: Extra stress tracks, specialized training, signature gear

## SETTING SUMMARY

**The World**: 93% ocean. Massive platform cities. Corporate control. Stratified by depth and elevation.

**The Tech**: Neural interfaces. Body modification normalized. AI assistants and emergent consciousness. Surveillance everywhere.

**The Tone**: Gene Wolfe meets Mr. Robot. Elegant paranoia. Competent characters under existential pressure.

**The Chrome**: Everyone's augmented. Every implant shifts your stress tracks. Power has a cost. Natural humans are rare and valuable.

**The Stakes**: Corporate dystopia. Resistance movements. Identity crisis. What do you sacrifice for capability? For survival? For meaning?

Full setting details in build documents and playbook materials.

## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

This game combines:
- **Fate**: Aspects, Fate points, fiction-first resolution
- **Powered by the Apocalypse**: Playbooks, agenda, fronts
- **Burning Wheel**: Let It Ride, say yes or roll the dice
- **Cyberpunk genre**: Chrome and consequences, corporate noir, identity crisis

**Core Assumptions**:
- Player characters are competent professionals
- Failure creates interesting complications, not dead ends
- Relationships and aspects drive drama
- Every mechanical choice reflects fictional positioning
- The table negotiates truth through conversation and dice

## THE KEY PRINCIPLE

When the dice stop rolling, what was decided happens.

No hedging. No "yes, but..." unless that was the agreed stakes. No negotiation after the fact.

You describe your intent. Stakes get set. Dice fall. Fiction changes.

Everything costs. Chrome shifts your tracks. Talents require Fate points. Consequences reshape you. You gain capability through sacrifice.

**Say yes or roll the dice. Change the fiction. Play to find out what happens.**
