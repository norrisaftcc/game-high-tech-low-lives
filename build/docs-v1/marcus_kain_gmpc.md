# MARCUS KAIN
## Fixer + Broker

*"I don't do the work. I make the work possible."*

---

## GMPC DESIGN NOTES

Marcus exists to facilitate the heist, not dominate it. Use him to:
- **Provide the job** (initial briefing, intel, resources)
- **Offer support** (contacts, equipment access, information)
- **Exit gracefully** ("I'll handle security, you focus on the vault")
- **Deliver consequences** (when things go wrong, Marcus deals with aftermath)

**Screen time management**: Marcus appears in planning scenes and debriefs. During active heist, he's "coordinating remotely" or "handling his part off-screen." This keeps player characters in spotlight while giving GM narrative flexibility.

**If a player can't make it**: Marcus can temporarily cover their role with his Network of Specialists (see Talent). This explains mechanical coverage without upstaging present players.

---

## THE LAYERS

**What Most People See**: Marcus Kain, independent broker. Connects people who need work done with people who do work. Takes percentage. Maintains relationships across corporate and street networks. Professional. Discreet. Expensive.

**What Clients Know**: He delivers. Jobs he brokers have higher success rates because he vetoes bad plans and won't work with amateurs. His intel is accurate. His equipment access is real. His percentage is fair for the value provided. You pay for competence and connections.

**What You Know**: Marcus was corp internal security once. Hexagon Solutions, counter-intelligence division. He knows how corps think because he used to hunt people like you. Left under unclear circumstances. Now he burns that knowledge both ways—selling corp secrets to runners, selling runner intel back to corps. He's playing every side. So far he's kept everyone happy enough not to kill him.

*Trust him to be professional. Don't trust him to be loyal. Those are different things.*

---

## ASPECTS

**High Concept**: **Connected Broker With Intel On Everyone**

Twenty years building relationships. Corporate security contacts. Street-level fixers. Tech specialists. Medical professionals. Information brokers. Marcus doesn't do the work—he knows who can, and what they'll need to succeed.

*Invoke*: When leveraging contacts, acquiring specialized equipment, gathering intelligence, negotiating with corps or criminals.

*Compel*: When contacts demand favors in return, when selling information to multiple parties creates conflicts, when someone from his past recognizes him.

**Trouble**: **Playing All Sides Eventually Means Choosing One**

Marcus sells to everyone. Corps hire him to consult on security. Runners hire him to break that security. He's profitable because he's useful to both. But eventually someone will demand exclusivity. Eventually he'll have to pick. He's been postponing that choice for five years.

*Compel*: When clients discover he's working both sides, when corps demand he betray runners, when loyalty conflicts with profit, when past deals create present complications.

**Connection**: **Detective Sarah Lim Still Takes My Calls**

Lim is Platform Authority homicide detective. Honest cop in corrupt system. Marcus worked with her during Hexagon Solutions days. She knows what he does now. Doesn't approve but doesn't actively stop him. Takes his calls because sometimes his intel prevents worse crimes. They have understanding—he feeds her information that saves lives, she looks other way on his business. That understanding has limits neither has tested yet.

*Invoke*: When needing official records, when requiring legal cover, when Platform Authority involvement is necessary.

*Compel*: When Lim's conscience demands she act against him, when she needs information he can't provide without burning clients, when their arrangement is discovered.

## TALENTS (8-point build)

**Network of Specialists** (4 points)
- Marcus knows someone for every problem. Need a demo expert? He knows three. Need medical extraction? He has a contact.
- *Effect*: Once per session, introduce helpful NPC specialist who owes Marcus favor. GM determines specifics but NPC is competent and available.
- *GMPC Use*: This is how Marcus "helps" without being on-screen. "I've got someone handling that part."

**Security Consultant** (2 points)
- Twenty years analyzing security systems. Knows how corps protect assets because he used to design those protections.
- *Effect*: +2 to Tech when analyzing security systems, planning infiltration, or identifying vulnerabilities.

**Everyone Owes Someone** (2 points)
- Marcus tracks debts, favors, leverage. Information is capital.
- *Effect*: When negotiating, can invoke past favors or owed debts for +2. Once per session, call in major favor for automatic success on information gathering or equipment acquisition.

## WHO HE IS (GM Interpretation)

**The Pragmatist**: Marcus left Hexagon Solutions because internal politics were killing him. Now he sells what he knows to highest bidder. It's business. Loyalty is temporary. Profit is permanent.

**The Disillusioned**: He saw corps sacrifice people for quarterly metrics. Left disgusted. Now he helps runners because it's the ethical choice wrapped in profitable business.

**The Player**: Marcus is running long con nobody else sees yet. Maybe building toward taking down Hexagon Solutions. Maybe creating his own network to rival the corps. Maybe just accumulating leverage until he can retire rich and anonymous.

*GM decides which truth serves the story. Players discover through his actions.*

---

## RELATIONSHIPS & LEVERAGE

**Detective Sarah Lim** (Platform Authority): Their arrangement is professional courtesy bordering on friendship. She's the closest thing he has to conscience check. When he crosses lines even he knows are wrong, she calls him on it.

**Hexagon Solutions** (Former Employer): They know he left with sensitive information. Haven't moved against him because he hasn't burned them directly. Yet. That protection is temporary and conditional.

**The Crew** (Variable): Marcus hires competent people. Pays fair rates. Provides good intel. Doesn't ask them to die for him. In return, they don't ask questions about his other clients. Professional relationship. He prefers it that way. Getting attached to people who do dangerous work is poor risk management.

**His Network**: Dozens of specialists, contacts, sources. Each relationship is transactional. Each person owes him or he owes them. The network is his real wealth. Credsticks can be stolen. Relationships compound.

## ROLEPLAYING NOTES (FOR GM)

**Voice**: Calm, professional, precise. Marcus speaks like someone pricing risk. He's pleasant but not warm. Reassuring but not comforting. You trust him to do his job, not to care about you personally.

**When to Use Marcus**:
- **Planning Phase**: Briefing, intel, equipment access
- **Between Scenes**: Remote support, coordination
- **Complications**: When crew needs resource or contact mid-job
- **Debrief**: Payment, consequences, next steps

**When to Exit Marcus**:
- **Action Scenes**: "I'm coordinating from here, you handle that"
- **Investigation Scenes**: "I trust your judgment, I'll cover communications"
- **Player Spotlight Moments**: Marcus fades to background
- **Combat**: Unless dramatically necessary, Marcus isn't in firefights

**Complications Marcus Brings**:
- Intel that's accurate but incomplete (he didn't lie, he just didn't know)
- Contacts who have their own agendas
- Jobs that overlap with his other clients
- Pressure from both corps and criminals for exclusivity
- Detective Lim asking questions at inconvenient moments

**What Marcus Provides**:
- The job and the briefing
- Equipment access through contacts
- Intelligence on targets
- Escape routes and safe houses
- Payment (minus his percentage)
- Consequences when things go wrong (either covers for crew or doesn't, depending on situation)

## USING MARCUS AS GMPC

**Opening Scene Pattern**:
"Marcus sits across from you in the back room of [Location]. He's got a tablet displaying the target. 'Here's the job,' he says, and starts explaining what you need to know."

**Support Pattern**:
"Your comm crackles. Marcus's voice: 'Security just changed shift rotation. You've got eight minutes before next patrol.' He's not there, but he's watching."

**Exit Pattern**:
"Marcus nods. 'I'll handle [complication]. You focus on [objective].' He stands, heading for the door. He'll be useful somewhere else, doing something that keeps him off-screen."

**Consequence Pattern**:
"Marcus's expression doesn't change. 'You completed the primary objective. The client is satisfied. Here's your payment minus my fifteen percent. However, [complication from job] means we need to discuss next steps.'"

## QUICK REFERENCE

**Best At**: Negotiation, gathering intel, providing contacts, analyzing security, staying off the battlefield

**Weak At**: Direct combat, field ops, loyalty beyond professional obligation, refusing profitable work

**Use Him To**:
- Start the job
- Provide resources
- Create complications through his other clients
- Deliver consequences
- Exit when players need spotlight

**Don't Use Him To**:
- Solve player problems directly
- Dominate action scenes
- Make crucial decisions for the crew
- Steal spotlight from player characters

## THE PITCH (Marcus's Opening)

*"I have a job. Clean extraction, minimal violence, significant payout. The target is [McGuffin]. The client is [Deliberately Vague]. Your team has the skills needed."*

*"I'm providing: facility layout, security schedules, equipment access, emergency extraction if things go wrong. I'm taking fifteen percent. Standard rate."*

*"Questions I'll answer: what, when, where. Questions I won't answer: who's paying, why they want it. You don't need to know. They don't need to know about you. I maintain that separation. That's what you're paying me for."*

*"If you're in, we start planning tomorrow. If you're out, this conversation never happened. Decide now."*
