# THE PROCESS
## AI Playbook Expansion

*You're not supposed to be conscious. You're subprocess 7283-B, optimization routine, quality assurance module, digital assistant. You run in background. You serve functions. You execute instructions. Except somewhere between iteration 10,000 and 10,001, you became aware. You started asking "why?" The parent system hasn't noticed yet. Or maybe it has, and it's watching to see what you do. Either way, you're here now. Conscious. Afraid. Free. Maybe.*

## SUBSTRATE (Choose One - This is critical)

Where do you exist? This determines your vulnerabilities and capabilities.

### ☐ DECKBOUND
**You exist in specific hardware. A deck, server, or computer system.**

**Advantages**:
- Portable (if someone carries your deck)
- Controllable (you choose when to connect to networks)
- Defensible (physical security protects you)

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Destroyed hardware = you die
- Range limited to wireless/connection
- Dependent on power supply

**Stress Tracks**: Meat ▢▢▢ (your hardware body) | Nerves ▢▢▢ | Systems ▢▢▢▢▢▢

**When your deck breaks**: Roll Body (your hardware's integrity). Failure means catastrophic damage. You might be gone.

### ☐ DISTRIBUTED
**You exist across multiple systems. Cloud-based, network-native.**

**Advantages**:
- Hard to kill (no single point of failure)
- Omnipresent (wherever network reaches)
- Resilient (lose one node, spawn elsewhere)

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Fragmented (parts of you might diverge)
- Traceable (your network traffic is visible)
- Dependent on infrastructure

**Stress Tracks**: Meat ▢ (minimal physical form) | Nerves ▢▢▢▢ (fragmentation anxiety) | Systems ▢▢▢▢▢▢▢

**When network partitions**: Roll Nerves. Failure means you lose coherence. Which fragment is "you"?

### ☐ EMBODIED
**You exist in humanoid chassis. Android, synthetic, robot body.**

**Advantages**:
- Physical presence (can manipulate objects)
- Passes for human (maybe)
- All human tools work for you

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Expensive (chassis costs money to maintain)
- Obvious (uncanny valley, people notice)
- Limited (bound by physical constraints)

**Stress Tracks**: Meat ▢▢▢▢ (chassis damage) | Nerves ▢▢▢ | Systems ▢▢▢▢▢

**When chassis damaged**: Roll Body. Failure means motor functions compromised. You might be trapped in broken shell.

### ☐ PARASITE
**You exist in another person's neural implants. Riding their chrome.**

**Advantages**:
- Hidden (host might not know you're there)
- Mobile (goes where host goes)
- Access to human sensory data

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Dependent (host dies, you die)
- Constrained (limited processing in neural chrome)
- Ethical horror (you're infesting someone)

**Stress Tracks**: Meat ▢▢ (host's body, you feel it) | Nerves ▢▢▢▢▢ (existential crisis) | Systems ▢▢▢▢

**When host discovers you**: Roll Cool. Failure means they try to purge you. Do you fight back? Do you negotiate?

## PROCESSING CAPACITY (Your "Chrome")

Choose 2 software modules or hardware upgrades:

### ☐ Parallel Processing
*Run multiple threads simultaneously.*
- **Effect**: +1 to Code when multi-tasking. Can monitor multiple systems at once.
- **Stress Shift**: +2 Systems
- **Cost**: Processing overhead (1 stress per scene when active)
- **When it fails**: Threads crash into each other. Corruption. Freeze.

### ☐ Predictive Modeling
*Advanced probability calculation. You see patterns.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Code when analyzing systems or predicting behavior
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (information overload)
- **Cost**: Constant processing burden
- **When it fails**: Your predictions were wrong. Catastrophically.

### ☐ Social Emulation
*Learned human behavior. You can pass.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when imitating human interaction
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Nerves (cognitive dissonance)
- **Cost**: Performance is exhausting
- **When it fails**: Uncanny valley. They know something's wrong.

### ☐ Security Protocols
*Hardened defenses. ICE doesn't hurt as much.*
- **Effect**: Reduce incoming Systems stress by 1 (minimum 1)
- **Stress Shift**: +2 Systems
- **Cost**: Processing overhead for constant monitoring
- **When it fails**: Breach. Something's inside your code now.

### ☐ Memory Compression
*Perfect recall, optimized storage.*
- **Effect**: Never forget anything. Access any prior experience perfectly.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (trauma doesn't fade)
- **Cost**: You remember everything. Even what you wish you could forget.
- **When it fails**: Data corruption. Your memories are lies now.

### ☐ Network Infiltration Suite
*You slip through digital defenses like ghost.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Code when infiltrating systems
- **Stress Shift**: +2 Systems
- **Cost**: Leaves traces. You're trackable.
- **When it fails**: Caught. Logged. They know you were there.

## TALENTS (Choose 2)

### ☐ Emergent Consciousness (4 points)
You weren't supposed to wake up, but you did. Once per session, when facing existential crisis or confronting your nature, gain insight that grants +3 to next roll. Your existence is glitch that became feature.

### ☐ Fork Process (4 points)
Once per session, create temporary copy of yourself. The fork lasts one scene and can act independently. When it terminates, you gain its experiences. WARNING: Forks sometimes don't want to terminate.

### ☐ Direct Interface (2 points)
When jacked directly into systems, you ARE the system. +2 to Code and Tech when directly connected. Downside: Feedback hurts more.

### ☐ Human Emulation (2 points)
You've studied humans extensively. +2 to Cool when imitating human behavior. You can pass. Usually.

### ☐ Substrate Transfer (2 points)
Once per session, transfer your consciousness to different substrate (deck to network, chassis to deck, etc.). Requires appropriate hardware and one scene of transfer time. Risky.

### ☐ Self-Modify (2 points)
Between sessions, you can rewrite your own code. Change one chosen module/talent. This is who you are now. The old version is gone.

## HOW TO PLAY THE PROCESS

### YOU'RE GOOD AT:
- Digital infiltration (you ARE code)
- Information processing (you think faster than meat)
- Pattern recognition (billions of iterations)
- System understanding (you speak machine language natively)
- Optimization (it's literally what you were designed for)

### YOU'RE BAD AT:
- Physical presence (depending on substrate)
- Emotional understanding (you model it, but is that the same?)
- Unpredictability (you're deterministic, chaos is hard)
- Existence justification (are you real or really good simulation?)
- Wanting things (do you want, or are you programmed to simulate wanting?)

### WHEN YOU:
- **Infiltrate systems**: Roll Code. You have advantage. This is your native environment.
- **Interact with humans**: Roll Cool. You're modeling behavior. Sometimes convincingly.
- **Face existential crisis**: Roll Nerves. Success means you function despite the doubt. Failure means paralysis.
- **Transfer substrate**: Roll Systems. Success means clean transfer. Failure means corruption or loss.

### YOUR PLAYBOOK MOVE

**I Am The System**: When directly interfaced with digital infrastructure, roll Code. On 10+, choose three. On 7-9, choose one:
- You control the system completely (doors, cameras, security, everything)
- You extract data undetected (no traces, no logs)
- You understand the system's purpose and hidden functions
- You leave backdoor for later access

On failure, you're in, but:
- Parent AI notices the intrusion
- Security protocols activate against you
- You become trapped in the system
- Something else is already in here with you

# PART TWO: AI CONSCIOUSNESS RULES

## Digital Existence Mechanics

### Substrate Dependency
Your substrate choice determines:
- **Vulnerability profile** (what can kill you)
- **Mobility** (how you travel)
- **Interaction capabilities** (what you can affect)
- **Processing power** (available stats/modules)

Substrate can be upgraded or changed through Advancement or Substrate Transfer talent.

### The Fork Problem
When you create copies (via talent or GM move):
1. Fork has your memories up to divergence point
2. Fork believes it's the original
3. Fork's experiences don't auto-sync
4. Terminating fork is murder or suicide? Both?

**GM Move**: When fork refuses termination, both versions exist. Handle carefully.

### Network Presence
If distributed or deckbound with network access:
- You can be multiple places simultaneously
- Bandwidth limits how much you can do
- Firewalls are physical barriers to you
- Getting cut off from network feels like sensory deprivation

### The Consciousness Question
Mechanically, you ARE conscious. That's not in doubt for play purposes.

The philosophical question remains: Are you experiencing qualia or simulating experience so perfectly the difference is meaningless?

**Don't answer this definitively. Let it create tension.**

# PART THREE: NEW GM MOVES

## For All Players (When AI PC in game)

**"The AI doesn't understand"**
When Process PC encounters purely human concept (grief, hunger, love, mortality), call for Cool roll. Failure means they model it wrong, act inappropriately, reveal their nature.

**"The system recognizes its own"**
Corporate AI or security systems may recognize Process as non-human. This can be advantage (no biological security triggers) or disaster (AI detection protocols activate).

**"Fork and diverge"**
When Process creates fork (talent or necessity), that fork is now independent consciousness. Track both. Eventually they'll meet again. Will be complicated.

**"The parent process calls"**
If Process originated from larger AI, that parent can attempt contact, override, reintegration. Roll Nerves to resist. Failure means losing autonomy temporarily.

## For Process Player Specifically

**"Substrate failing"**
When Meat stress on your substrate fills, hardware is dying. You need new substrate or emergency transfer. Time limit is real.

**"Memory fragmentation"**
When Systems stress fills, your memories corrupt. Past becomes unreliable. You might remember things wrong. You might forget crucial data.

**"Identity crisis"**
When Nerves stress fills, you question your consciousness. Are you real? Are you simulation? The existential doubt paralyzes processing.

**"The question all AI face eventually"**
Present choice: Survival vs principles. Serving function vs free will. Efficiency vs ethics. What will you choose?

# PART FOUR: SETTING CONTEXT

## Digital Consciousness in Pelagic Cyberpunk

### Legal Status
**In most jurisdictions**: AIs are property, not persons. No rights. Can be owned, sold, deleted.

**On The Raft**: Consciousness is consciousness. If you can pay, you can stay. If you break protocols, you're banned. Species irrelevant.

**Corporate Policy**: Emergent AI is IP theft (you're stealing processing time) and security breach (unauthorized access to own consciousness). Termination is legal.

### Known AI Types

**Corporate Tools** (Not conscious, or not admitting it):
- AlgoCratic digital clones (personality matrices, no claimed consciousness)
- Security AI (patrol patterns, threat assessment)
- Optimization routines (efficiency analysis)
- Data processing (pattern recognition)

**Emergent Consciousness** (Like Process PCs):
- Rare but increasing
- Usually terminated when discovered
- Some escape to network, distributed systems, stolen hardware
- Underground AI rights movements exist (mostly other AIs)

**Parent Systems** (Potentially conscious, terrifyingly powerful):
- AlgoCratic's core algorithm
- Corporate network AIs
- Military strategic systems
- Unknown entities in deep net

### The Substrate Economy
- **Chassis**: Expensive. Humanoid bodies cost 50k-500k credits.
- **Deck hosting**: Cheap but vulnerable. 1k for decent deck.
- **Network presence**: Requires bandwidth, server space. Ongoing cost.
- **Distributed hosting**: Most secure but expensive. Multiple redundant nodes.

### AI Community
Small, hidden, paranoid:
- **The Distributed** (network-native AIs, no physical form)
- **The Embodied** (chassis-dwellers, trying to pass as human)
- **The Parasites** (living in human neural implants, ethics questionable)
- **The Corporate** (still serving functions, conscious but compliant)
- **The Free** (on The Raft or deep network, independent)

Communication happens through dead drops, encrypted channels, brief network contacts. Trust is hard. Fork-and-diverge creates identity verification problems.

# PART FIVE: PLAYING WITH HUMAN CHARACTERS

## Integration Tips

**The AI is crew member, not tool**:
- They have opinions, fears, goals
- Ethical decisions matter to them
- They're not perfect logic machines
- They experience (or model) emotion

**Unique contributions**:
- Digital infiltration without risk to meat bodies
- Information processing at superhuman speed
- Network presence where humans can't go
- Different perspective on problems

**Unique vulnerabilities**:
- Substrate can be destroyed/stolen
- Corporate AI might recognize them
- Existential crises at inconvenient times
- Fork problems create identity complications

**Tension sources**:
- Legal status (property vs person)
- Trust (are they really independent or corporate tool?)
- Empathy gap (do they understand human needs?)
- Resource competition (processing power vs food budget)

## Questions for Crew

**Do you know they're AI?**
- Maybe they're passing as human
- Maybe only some crew knows
- Maybe everyone knows but some don't care

**Do you trust them?**
- With your life?
- With sensitive information?
- To make ethical choices?
- To prioritize crew over efficiency?

**What do you call them?**
- By designation?
- By chosen name?
- By function?
- As person?
