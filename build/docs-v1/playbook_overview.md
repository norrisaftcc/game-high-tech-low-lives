# COMPLETE PLAYBOOK SET OVERVIEW

## AVAILABLE PLAYBOOKS

Each playbook represents a distinctive approach to cyberpunk crime. Choose the one that matches your preferred role in the crew.

---

## ✅ THE INFILTRATOR (Complete)
**Core Identity**: Corporate access, stolen credentials, finding vulnerabilities  
**Primary Stat**: Code +3  
**Stress Pattern**: Balanced → Heavy Systems after chrome  
**Key Tension**: Multiple identities, which one is real?  
**Best At**: Getting in, finding weaknesses, maintaining cover  
**Worst At**: Direct confrontation, trusting anyone  

**Play this if you want**: Layered identity, corporate espionage, teaching through exploitation, surveillance cat-and-mouse

---

## ✅ THE INFLUENCER (Complete)
**Core Identity**: Streamed competence, corporate sponsors, performance merged with skill  
**Primary Stat**: Tech +3, Cool +2  
**Stress Pattern**: Heavily chromed (6+ Systems), low Meat/Nerves  
**Key Tension**: Performance vs authenticity, followers as asset and liability  
**Best At**: Technical work, social engineering through fame, looking good while working  
**Worst At**: Anonymity, going off-grid, separating personal from professional  

**Play this if you want**: Attention economy mechanics, sponsor obligations, audience as resource and complication

---

## ✅ THE NOMAD (Complete)
**Core Identity**: Boat captain, smuggler, knows the routes  
**Primary Stat**: Reflexes +3  
**Stress Pattern**: Minimal chrome, boat is separate character  
**Key Tension**: Boat is home and liability, freedom vs maintenance costs  
**Best At**: Piloting, navigation, evasion, combat when necessary  
**Worst At**: Staying in one place, trusting corps, saying no to refugees  

**Play this if you want**: Vehicle-based play, maritime freedom, ex-military background, people smuggling

---

## 🔲 THE FIXER
**Core Identity**: Broker, connector, knows everyone  
**Primary Stat**: Cool +3  
**Stress Pattern**: Minimal chrome, emphasis on Nerves (managing relationships)  
**Key Tension**: Playing all sides means eventually choosing one  
**Best At**: Negotiation, gathering intel, providing contacts, equipment access  
**Worst At**: Direct action, loyalty beyond professional obligation  

**Distinctive Mechanics**:
- **Network of Specialists** talent (introduce helpful NPCs)
- **Everyone Owes Someone** tracking system
- Can broker jobs for other players
- Compels around conflicting client interests

**Play this if you want**: Social manipulation, being the crew's handler, juggling obligations, information broker role

---

## 🔲 THE RIGGER  
**Core Identity**: Hardware specialist, physical security expert, loves machines  
**Primary Stat**: Tech +3  
**Stress Pattern**: Chrome focused on tool integration, drones/remotes  
**Key Tension**: Better with machines than people  
**Best At**: Bypassing physical security, drone operation, hardware hacking, trap detection  
**Worst At**: Social situations, trusting meat over machine  

**Distinctive Mechanics**:
- **Drone Fleet** (starts with 3 specialized drones)
- **Direct Interface** (control machines like extension of body)
- Can "see" through cameras and sensors
- Systems stress represents both personal chrome and controlled devices

**Play this if you want**: Remote operation, swarm tactics, being the team's eyes, hardware focus over wetware

---

## 🔲 THE MEDIC
**Core Identity**: Street doctor, patch people up, ask no questions  
**Primary Stat**: Body +2, Tech +3  
**Stress Pattern**: Moderate chrome (medical enhancement), high Nerves capacity (seen too much)  
**Key Tension**: Hippocratic oath vs. street reality  
**Best At**: Healing, stabilizing, chrome installation/repair, reading biosigns  
**Worst At**: Letting people die even when tactical, staying detached  

**Distinctive Mechanics**:
- Can clear others' stress/consequences
- **Triage** move (who lives, who doesn't)
- Chrome maintenance for crew
- Consequences from losing patients

**Play this if you want**: Support role, moral weight of healing, "no questions asked" clinic, chrome surgeon

---

## 🔲 THE ENFORCER
**Core Identity**: Corporate muscle, street soldier, professional violence  
**Primary Stat**: Body +3, Reflexes +2  
**Stress Pattern**: Combat chrome (subdermal armor, reflex boost), low Nerves  
**Key Tension**: Following orders vs personal code  
**Best At**: Combat, intimidation, protection, breaking things efficiently  
**Worst At**: Subtlety, de-escalation, situations requiring social finesse  

**Distinctive Mechanics**:
- **Combat Veteran** (bonus in extended contests)
- **Presence** (intimidation without violence)
- Can absorb hits meant for others
- Corporate or gang affiliation with obligations

**Play this if you want**: Heavy combat focus, bodyguard role, organized crime ties, "I'm good at violence" character

---

## 🔲 THE FACE
**Core Identity**: Social engineer, con artist, charm and lies  
**Primary Stat**: Cool +3  
**Stress Pattern**: Cosmetic chrome (enhancement not replacement), Nerves-focused  
**Key Tension**: Every relationship is transaction vs actual connections forming  
**Best At**: Talking your way in/out, reading people, creating cover stories, manipulation  
**Worst At**: Technical work, physical confrontation, maintaining relationships after burn  

**Distinctive Mechanics**:
- **Perfect Lie** (roll Cool to fabricate convincing identity)
- **Read Intent** (see what people want before they say it)
- **Burn Bridges** (nuclear option, succeed but relationship destroyed)
- Multiple identity documentation

**Play this if you want**: Pure social manipulation, con artist gameplay, no technical skills, silver tongue is weapon

---

## 🔲 THE HACKER
**Core Identity**: Pure netrunner, lives in cyberspace, meat is just life support  
**Primary Stat**: Code +4 (only playbook that starts this high)  
**Stress Pattern**: Maximum Systems (8+ boxes), minimal Meat (often bedridden)  
**Key Tension**: Detachment from physical reality, addiction to the net  
**Best At**: Deep dives, ICE breaking, data theft, cyber warfare  
**Worst At**: Physical anything, social interaction, leaving their rig  

**Distinctive Mechanics**:
- **Netrunning Extended Contests** (special rules)
- **Black ICE** (lethal countermeasures)
- Can operate from remote location during jobs
- Meat body vulnerability while jacked in

**Play this if you want**: Pure cyber focus, minimal physical presence, "console cowboy" archetype, detachment from reality

## CHOOSING YOUR PLAYBOOK

### For Heist Crews, You Need:
- **Access** (Infiltrator, Face, or Influencer)
- **Technical** (Rigger, Hacker, or Influencer)
- **Extraction** (Nomad or Enforcer)
- **Support** (Fixer, Medic, or Face)

### For Stat Coverage:
- At least one high Code (Infiltrator, Hacker)
- At least one high Tech (Rigger, Influencer, Medic)
- At least one high Cool (Face, Fixer, Influencer)
- At least one high Reflexes (Nomad, Enforcer)

### For Chrome Philosophy:
- **Heavy Chrome**: Influencer, Hacker, Rigger
- **Moderate Chrome**: Infiltrator, Enforcer, Medic
- **Minimal Chrome**: Nomad, Face, Fixer

### For Narrative Style:
- **Identity Play**: Infiltrator, Face, Influencer
- **Freedom Seeking**: Nomad, Hacker
- **Professional Code**: Enforcer, Fixer, Medic
- **Performance/Audience**: Influencer
- **Technical Mastery**: Rigger, Hacker

## FORMAT CONSISTENCY

All playbooks follow same structure:

1. **Flavor Text** (who you are, what you do)
2. **Name** (suggestions or create your own)
3. **Look** (visual choices)
4. **Stats** (pre-assigned for archetype)
5. **Starting Stress** (before chrome modifications)
6. **Chrome** (choose X, each modifies stress tracks)
7. **Talents** (choose 2 from 8-point budget)
8. **Aspects** (High Concept, Trouble, Connection with examples)
9. **Starting Gear** (essential equipment)
10. **Example Consequences** (Mild/Moderate/Severe for each stress track)
11. **How To Play** (good at, bad at, when you roll)
12. **Playbook Move** (signature mechanical advantage)
13. **Relationships** (Phase Four connections)
14. **Advancement** (what you gain when arcs resolve)
15. **Questions To Answer** (discover through play)

**Print-and-play ready**: Everything needed on 2-4 pages, no external reference required.
