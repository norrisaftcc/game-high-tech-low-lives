# HIGH-TECH-LOW-LIVES
## A Cyberpunk Heist RPG

*You don't have many lives left. Make them count.*

---

## WHAT THIS GAME IS

You're criminals, outcasts, and desperate people operating in the margins of corporate power. You have advanced chrome, illegal skills, and dangerous connections. You take jobs that pay well because you need the money to survive, maintain your augmentations, and maybe—just maybe—get out.

But your chrome is slowly killing you. Your stress is accumulating. Your consequences are piling up. You're running out of time.

**The question:** How will you spend the life you have left?

---

## THE SETTING

**Golgotha Mining Colony**: Toxic atmosphere, brutal working conditions, three-year contracts most don't survive. Corporate debt prison where you dig so they can fly. Underground chrome art scene. Deep levels where corps don't go.

**Sidereal Station**: Orbital arcology at Lagrange-4. Zero-g manufacturing, executive retreats, corporate paradise. Sanitized and controlled. Service staff hidden from view. Abandoned military sections.

Between them: Transit routes, smuggling channels, corporate checkpoints, and people trying to survive.

---

## WHAT YOU NEED TO PLAY

**3-5 Players**: One GM, 2-4 players

**Materials**: 
- Four Fate dice per player (4dF: showing +, -, or blank)
- Character playbooks (pick one each)
- Core moves sheet (everyone references this)
- Pencils and paper
- 10-15 tokens (coins, poker chips) for Fate points

**Time**: 
- Session Zero: 90 minutes (character creation, setup)
- Regular sessions: 2-4 hours

---

## HOW TO PLAY

### 1. PICK YOUR PLAYBOOK

Each playbook grants different narrative authority:

**THE INFILTRATOR** - Authority over access and information
*"I've been here before. I left myself a backdoor."*

**THE INFLUENCER** - Authority over attention and audience
*"My 50,000 followers are watching this right now."*

**THE NOMAD** - Authority over mobility and routes
*"I know a way through the storms that corporate patrols can't follow."*

**THE FIXER** - Authority over connections and specialists
*"I know someone who can handle this."*

---

### 2. BUILD YOUR CHARACTER

Follow your playbook:
1. Choose your stats (pre-assigned per playbook)
2. Choose chrome (2-3 pieces, shifts stress tracks)
3. Choose talents (2 from your list)
4. Write three aspects (High Concept, Trouble, Connection)
5. Calculate stress tracks (modified by chrome)
6. Take starting gear
7. Establish relationships with other PCs

**Total time:** 30-40 minutes

---

### 3. LEARN THE CORE MOVES

Reference the moves sheet for:
- When to roll dice (when opposed, uncertain, or failure is interesting)
- How aspects work (invoke for +2, accept compels for Fate points)
- Basic moves (everyone can use these)
- Stress and consequences (how you break)
- Fate point economy (gain from compels, spend on invokes)

**Key principle:** Say yes or roll the dice. Default to "yes" unless there's opposition or uncertainty.

---

### 4. USE EMBEDDED COMMANDS

**Don't ask permission. State what's happening.**

Instead of:
- ❌ "Can I hack this?"
- ❌ "Do I know someone here?"

Embed the truth:
- ✅ "**As I hack this**, I notice their security is outdated..."
- ✅ "**When I scan the crowd**, I recognize my old Sidereal contact..."

**The pattern:**
1. Action (what you're doing)
2. Embedded truth (what you notice/know/feel)
3. Opening (for GM to respond with complication)

This keeps fiction moving and uses your playbook authority naturally.

---

### 5. MANAGE YOUR RESOURCES

**Stress Tracks:**
- Meat (physical damage)
- Nerves (psychological strain)
- Systems (chrome and gear damage)

**Consequences:**
- Mild (2 shifts, clears after one scene)
- Moderate (4 shifts, requires help to clear)
- Severe (6 shifts, permanent until bought off)

**Fate Points:**
- Start with 3 per session
- Gain by accepting compels
- Spend to invoke aspects (+2 or reroll)

**Chrome:**
- Requires monthly maintenance (credits)
- Breaks when Systems stress fills
- When broken, check playbook for effects

## YOUR FIRST SESSION

### Session Zero (90 minutes):

1. **Explain premise** (15 min)
   - Cyberpunk heists, corporate dystopia, running out of time
   - Golgotha and Sidereal as primary locations
   - Chrome as identity and cost

2. **Choose playbooks** (20 min)
   - Each player picks and reads their playbook
   - Explain to group what they can declare

3. **Build characters** (30 min)
   - Follow playbook structure
   - Choose chrome (significant choices)
   - Write aspects
   - Calculate stress tracks

4. **Establish relationships** (10 min)
   - Phase Four from playbooks
   - Who knows who, what complications exist

5. **Review moves sheet** (15 min)
   - When to roll
   - How aspects work
   - Fate point economy
   - Embedded command pattern

---

### First Heist Session (2-4 hours):

**Start in media res:**
- "You're infiltrating Sidereal Station during the corporate pickleball tournament..."
- "You're fleeing Golgotha security through toxic dust storms..."
- "You're meeting your fixer at The Raft, and they have a job..."

**Call for rolls when:**
- Success is uncertain
- Someone opposes the action
- Failure would be interesting

**Offer compels early:**
- "Your Trouble complicates this situation. Take a Fate point?"
- "Your Connection means they recognize you. Take a Fate point?"

**Let players use signature moves:**
- Infiltrator's Familiar Ground
- Influencer's The Stream
- Nomad's Nobody Stops Me
- Fixer's I Know Someone Who Can Help

**Introduce stress and consequences:**
- Combat, environmental hazards, social pressure
- Show how stress accumulates
- Demonstrate consequence naming

## THE THREE-LEVEL TITLE

**HIGH-TECH-LOW-LIVES** means:

1. **High-Tech** - Advanced augmentation, cutting-edge systems, orbital stations
2. **Lowlifes** - Criminals, outcasts, margin-dwellers
3. **Low Lives** - Running out of time, burning out, few lives left

Every job costs you something. Every consequence accumulates. Every session, your chrome degrades a little more. You're not building toward retirement.

**You're burning bright before you burn out.**

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

### Make The Implicit Explicit
- Players know what they can declare (playbook authority)
- Players know when to roll (moves sheet)
- Players know how to affect outcomes (Fate points)
- Nothing is hidden

### Show Them The Levers
- "I can invoke this aspect for +2"
- "I can use this talent automatically"
- "I can accept this compel for a Fate point"
- Transparency enables informed choices

### Distribute Authority Fairly
- Each playbook grants different authority
- No playbook is more powerful
- Everyone contributes uniquely
- Niche protection ensures spotlight

### Reward Accepting Complications
- Compels grant Fate points
- Fate points enable invokes
- More drama = better story + more power
- Players choose when to accept complications

---

## WHAT MAKES THIS GAME WORK

**Mechanical clarity**: Every rule does specific work  
**Narrative transparency**: Players know their authority  
**Distributed control**: Everyone contributes uniquely  
**Meaningful failure**: Complications create story  
**Economic incentives**: Rewarded for accepting drama  
**Setting grounding**: Premise built into world  

**The game loop:**
- Players declare using playbook authority
- GM introduces complications
- Dice resolve uncertainty
- Consequences accumulate
- Story emerges from negotiation
