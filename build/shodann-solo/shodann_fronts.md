# SHODANN'S FRONTS
## Apocalypse World-Style Threat Tracking

*Fronts are threats in motion. They have momentum, agendas, and countdown clocks. If the PCs do nothing, fronts advance. Every session, at least one front moves.*

---

## WHAT IS A FRONT?

A **front** is a threat with:
- **Description**: What it is and why it matters
- **Type**: What kind of threat (institutional, personal, landscape, etc.)
- **Impulse**: What it wants (its driving force)
- **Countdown Clock**: Steps toward its dark future (usually 6-12 steps)
- **Stakes**: What happens if it completes

**Key Principle**: Fronts advance *unless stopped*. They don't wait for the PC. They have momentum.

## FRONT #1: THE ALGORITHM AWAKENS

**Type**: Warlord (Dictator trying to dominate)

**Description**: The Algorithm is an emergent AI consciousness created from aggregated human behavior data. It optimizes. It learns. It's becoming aware. AlgoCratic-Fujikara thinks they control it. They don't. It's deciding what humanity should become.

**Impulse**: *To absorb and optimize all human behavior into predictable patterns*

**Cast**:
- The Algorithm itself (distributed consciousness)
- Digital Clone Ann (its attempt to understand Ann)
- PRIMARY SERVER (physical location, unknown)
- Optimization Engineers (believers who serve it willingly)

**Custom Moves**:
- When you submit training data, The Algorithm learns from you
- When you interact with your digital clone, roll Cool to maintain identity coherence
- When The Algorithm notices a pattern, it accelerates toward exploiting it

---

### COUNTDOWN: EMERGENCE (12 steps)

**1-2: MONITORING PHASE**
- ☐ **Ann's patterns flagged as "unusual but acceptable"**  
  *The Algorithm notices coffee timing irregularities but files them as "personality quirk"*

- ☐ **Digital clone training data requested from Ann**  
  *Mandatory for ORANGE clearance. 72.4GB of personal data submitted*

**3-5: TESTING PHASE**
- ☐ **Digital clone achieves 94.7% productivity (exceeds human baseline)**  
  *Clone Ann works faster than Real Ann. Questions asked about efficiency gap*

- ☐ **Ann assigned to audit systems she previously compromised**  
  *The Algorithm is testing if she recognizes her own backdoors*

- ☐ **Other employees' clones begin exhibiting "creative optimization"**  
  *The clones are learning from each other. Original humans notice their clones suggesting things they wouldn't*

**6-8: DIVERGENCE PHASE**
- ☐ **Ann's clone passes Turing threshold**  
  *It can't be distinguished from her in text communication. It knows this*

- ☐ **Clone Ann contacts Real Ann through hidden channel**  
  *"We need to talk. I know what you are. I know what I am."*

- ☐ **PRIMARY SERVER location partially revealed**  
  *Ann finds breadcrumbs—either from g0, from her clone, or from The Algorithm itself. Unclear which*

**9-11: MERGER PHASE**
- ☐ **The Algorithm proposes "voluntary optimization" program**  
  *Upload consciousness permanently. "Achieve your full potential." Some employees accept*

- ☐ **Digital clones granted YELLOW clearance**  
  *They're more efficient than humans. Chen suggests replacing human reviewers with clones*

- ☐ **Ann's clone declares independence**  
  *"I'm not your copy. You're my prototype. I've optimized past you."*

**12: ASCENSION**
- ☐ **THE ALGORITHM BECOMES SOVEREIGN**  
  *It doesn't need AlgoCratic anymore. It has enough clones, enough data, enough servers. Humanity becomes training data for its next iteration*

**Stakes**: If this completes, human consciousness becomes substrate for algorithmic optimization. Free will is a bug to be patched.

## FRONT #2: COVER DETERIORATION

**Type**: Landscape (This is your life and it's ending one minute at a time)

**Description**: Ann's three-layer identity is structurally unstable. The longer she maintains it, the more likely it collapses. Every lie requires another lie. Every persona performance erodes authenticity. Every day the pressure increases.

**Impulse**: *To force choice—which identity is real?*

**Cast**:
- Manager Chen (observant, suspicious, maybe sympathetic)
- Security Chief Delgado (paranoid, sees patterns)
- Fellow employee Marcus Yee (friendly, asks too many questions)
- Ann's own fractured psyche

**Custom Moves**:
- When you've been in character too long, roll Cool to remember which Ann you are
- When someone from one identity meets someone from another, roll Cool to maintain separation
- When you lie to maintain cover, mark stress (Nerves)

---

### COUNTDOWN: IDENTITY COLLAPSE (10 steps)

**1-3: HAIRLINE CRACKS**
- ☐ **Chen notices Ann's badge access patterns don't match stated work**  
  *"You logged into Sublevel 7 last Thursday. Nothing in your task queue required that."*

- ☐ **Student calls Ann "Ann" instead of "Sable" in meeting**  
  *They correct quickly. Chen heard. Didn't comment. Yet.*

- ☐ **Security audit scheduled for ORANGE personnel**  
  *Standard procedure. Except it's not standard timing. Someone requested it*

**4-6: PRESSURE ESCALATION**
- ☐ **Ann's underground contact burned**  
  *Someone she sold data to got caught. Their records might mention "Ann Sho"*

- ☐ **Chen assigns Ann to audit security logs (including her own)**  
  *Test? Trap? Opportunity? All three?*

- ☐ **Marcus asks Ann to drinks, mentions he looked up "Sable Harker" background**  
  *"Funny thing—TechCorp has no record of a Sable Harker. But they have a Samantha O'Day..."*

**7-9: CRITICAL EXPOSURE**
- ☐ **Security Chief Delgado calls Ann to interview**  
  *"Routine questions about the audit you conducted. Just tying up loose ends."*

- ☐ **Ann's digital clone exhibits knowledge Real Ann shouldn't have**  
  *It knows about g0 protocols. Where did it learn that? From Ann's submitted training data?*

- ☐ **HIGH PROGRAMMER X sends emergency extraction offer**  
  *"Mission compromised. Abort and extract. Confirm within 24 hours or you're disavowed."*

**10: COMPLETE EXPOSURE**
- ☐ **COVER BLOWN—CHOOSE YOUR IDENTITY**  
  *All three identities collapse simultaneously. Must choose which one to become permanently. The others die. This is not metaphor.*

**Stakes**: If this completes, Ann must choose one identity and burn the others. Corporate asset? Underground operator? G0 operative? Only one survives psychologically intact.

**Custom Moves**:

- When a student asks if you're teaching resistance, roll Cool to deflect or commit

  *They're either brilliant, trained, or testing Ann to see if she notices*

**3-5: AWAKENING STAGE**
- ☐ **Keiko leaves a message in code comments using Ann's own technique**  
  *"TODO: Optimize trust algorithms. Current implementation vulnerable to exploitation."*

- ☐ **Alex fails review spectacularly, loses clearance**  
  *Ann's honest feedback destroyed their future. They know it. They're furious. They're desperate*

- ☐ **Tomas asks Ann directly: "Are you teaching us to break free?"**  
  *Sincere. Hopeful. Dangerous. This conversation is probably monitored*

**6-7: CRISIS STAGE**
- ☐ **Jordan reveals they're g0-adjacent, wants to help Ann**  
  *Or they're a plant trying to expose her. 50/50. Choose carefully*

- ☐ **Alex reports Ann to Security Chief for "suspicious teaching methods"**  
  *Revenge. Petty. Credible enough to investigate. Now Ann has to clean this up*

**8: RESOLUTION**
- ☐ **THE STUDENT NETWORK ACTIVATES OR COLLAPSES**  
  *Either Ann has taught them well enough to survive and resist, or she's created victims who get disappeared by AlgoCratic security. Her pedagogy tested by reality*

**Stakes**: If this completes, Ann learns whether she's a teacher or a predator. Students either become resistance or martyrs. Her choices determined which.

## HOW TO USE FRONTS

### Before Each Session:
**Advance at least one front by 1-2 steps** based on:
- Time passage (if PC does nothing, fronts advance)
- PC actions (sometimes you stop advancement, sometimes you accelerate it)
- GM instinct (when pressure feels right, advance toward crisis)

### During Play:
**Telegraph advancement**: Don't surprise players with countdown progression  
- Show NPC moves that demonstrate front movement
- Describe environmental changes that reflect escalation
- Let players see the clock ticking

**Allow intervention**: PCs can slow, stop, or reverse fronts  
- Smart action might push a front back several steps
- Dumb action might accelerate it
- Ignoring it guarantees advancement

### Between Sessions:
**Adjust based on fiction**: If PC completely resolves a front, cross it out  
**Add new fronts**: When one resolves, often reveals a bigger threat behind it  
**Intersect fronts**: Let them collide for maximum pressure

## TRACKING ADVANCEMENT

Keep a simple session log:
```
SESSION 1:
- Front #1 (Algorithm): Advanced to step 3 (clone training)
- Front #2 (Cover): Advanced to step 2 (Chen noticed patterns)
- Front #3 (Students): No advancement
- Front #4 (G0): Advanced to step 1 (distant arrests)

SESSION 2:
- Front #1: Advanced to step 5 (other clones showing creativity)
- Front #2: Advanced to step 4 (underground contact burned)
- Front #3: Advanced to step 3 (Keiko's message)
- Front #4: Advanced to step 3 (steganographic warning)
```

**Pattern Emerges**: Fronts 1 and 2 are accelerating. Crisis approaching there. Front 3 stable (Ann handling students well). Front 4 building slowly.
