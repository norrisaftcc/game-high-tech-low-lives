## SECTION A: INSTANT START

**You're playing pre-generated Shodann.** Here's everything you need:

### Your Character (30 seconds)
**Name**: Ann Sho (aka Sable Harker, aka Samantha O'Day)
**Role**: Corporate spy, g0 resistance operative, teacher
**High Concept**: "g0 Operative Playing Three Roles at Once"
**Trouble**: "Which Ann Sho Is Real?"

**Stats**:
- Code +3 (hacking, systems)
- Cool +1 (composure, lies)
- Tech +1 (hardware, tools)
- Reflexes +1 (speed, combat)
- Body +0 (strength, toughness)

**Stress Tracks**:
- MEAT: [□ □ □]
- NERVES: [□ □ □ □]
- SYSTEMS: [□ □ □]

**Fate Points**: 3 (start each session with 3)

### Your Situation (30 seconds)
You've been undercover at AlgoCratic-Fujikara for 4 weeks. You teach corporate code review by day, run resistance ops by night. You have three identities:
- **Sable Harker** - Corporate employee (your cover)
- **Ann Sho** - Underground data broker (your street ID)
- **Samantha O'Day** - True self (g0 operative)

Four threats are advancing toward crisis. You can't stop them all.

### First Scene Setup (1 minute)
**TIME**: Morning, Day 28 of infiltration
**PLACE**: AlgoCratic campus café, 6:47 AM
**MISSION**: Leave steganographic message for your handler via coffee order
**PROBLEM**: Manager Chen just walked in. She's never here this early.

The barista looks at you: "The usual, Sable?"

**What do you do?**

### Your Options (Choose One)

**A) Order the usual (contains coded message)**
- Roll Code +3 to make it natural
- If successful: Message sent, Chen doesn't notice
- If fail: Message sent, but Chen is curious
- **Risk**: She might notice the pattern

**B) Chat with Chen, abort dead drop**
- No roll needed, automatically safe
- Build relationship with Chen
- **Cost**: Handler doesn't get this week's intel

**C) Try to do both (risky)**
- Roll Code +3 AND Cool +1, both at -1 penalty
- If both succeed: Message sent, Chen trust built
- If one fails: Mixed results (GM interprets)
- **High risk, high reward**

**D) Change your order (sacrifice mission)**
- No roll, perfectly safe
- No message sent, perfect cover maintained
- **Cost**: Intel about vulnerabilities not transmitted

### Rolling Dice (30 seconds)
Roll 4 Fate Dice (4dF). Each die shows +, -, or blank.

**Don't have Fate dice?**
- Use online: https://perchance.org/fate-dice
- Or 4d6: 1-2=minus, 3-4=blank, 5-6=plus

**Add your stat to the roll**:
- Code +3: Need total of 3+ to succeed
- Cool +1: Need total of 3+ to succeed

**Example**: Roll shows [+][-][blank][+] = +1. Code +3 + 1 = 4 (Success!)

### Resolution (1 minute)
Based on your choice and roll, narrate what happens. Then:

**If you ordered usual and succeeded**:
→ Message sent. Chen noticed nothing. Mark: Front #4 (G0 Network) advances +1 (handler received intel)

**If you ordered usual and failed**:
→ Message sent. Chen comments: "You're very particular about your coffee timing, Sable." Mark: Front #2 (Cover Deterioration) advances +1

**If you chatted with Chen**:
→ She mentions she's impressed by your work ethic. No fronts advance. You learn: She's testing ORANGE personnel for YELLOW promotions.

**If you tried both and mixed results**:
→ Choose which succeeded. The other creates complication.

**If you changed order**:
→ Cover perfect. Chen smiles. No fronts advance. Mission intel lost this week.

### What Happens Next (30 seconds)

Chen finishes her coffee and says: *"Sable, I need to see you this afternoon. Performance review. Nothing to worry about."*

She smiles. It might be friendly. It might not be.

**You just played your first scene.**

### Step 2: Choose Your Trouble (30 seconds)
Pick one or create your own:

- [ ] "Which Ann Sho Is Real?" (identity crisis)
- [ ] "Students Deserve Truth Even If It Destroys Them" (moral burden)
- [ ] "The Algorithm Knows My Patterns" (surveillance pressure)
- [ ] "Can't Trust Anyone, Including Myself" (paranoia)

**Write it down.**

### Step 3: Assign Stats (1 minute)
You have: **+3, +2, +1, +1, +0** to distribute among:

**Code** - Hacking, systems, security (usually +3 for Shodann)
**Cool** - Composure, lies, social engineering
**Tech** - Hardware, tools, repair
**Reflexes** - Speed, dodge, combat
**Body** - Strength, toughness, endurance

**Recommended for infiltrator**:
- Code +3
- Cool +2
- Tech +1
- Reflexes +1
- Body +0

**Calculate stress** (based on stats):
- MEAT boxes: 3 + Body stat
- NERVES boxes: 3 + Cool stat
- SYSTEMS boxes: 3 (always 3)

### Step 4: Choose One Crew Connection (1 minute)
Who helped you before? Pick one:

**A) The Nomad (Boat Captain)**
*They extracted you when infiltration went wrong. You owe them.*
**Aspect**: "[Nomad Name] Gets Me Out When Things Go Wrong"

**B) The Rigger (Hardware Specialist)**
*They built you undetectable surveillance tools.*
**Aspect**: "[Rigger Name] Builds Things The Algorithm Can't See"

**C) The Face (Social Engineer)**
*They manufactured your cover identity's social proof.*
**Aspect**: "[Face Name] Made Sable Harker Real—For a Price"

**Write the aspect down.**

**Protective Deceiver**: Keep them safe by keeping them ignorant
**Exploitative Pragmatist**: Use them for mission, minimize attachment

**This choice affects Scene Two.** Remember which you picked.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**"I don't have dice"**
→ Use phone app, online roller, or coin flips (heads=+, tails=-)

**"What if I pick wrong?"**
→ No wrong choices. Every choice advances different fronts. That's the story.

**"How do I know what to do?"**
→ Ask "What would Ann do given her High Concept and Trouble?" Trust your instincts.

**"This is confusing"**
→ Just play Scene One. The system teaches itself through play.
