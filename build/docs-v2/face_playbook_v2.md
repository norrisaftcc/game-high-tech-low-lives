## CHROME (Choose 2)

You use chrome to be more convincing, not more powerful.

### ☐ Social Analysis Suite
*Read micro-expressions, vocal stress, pupil dilation. Know what people feel before they do.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when reading people's emotions, detecting lies, or assessing social dynamics
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (everyone is transparent, you can't unsee it)
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: You're reading people wrong, seeing deception everywhere. Paranoia. -2 to social rolls.

### ☐ Voice Modulation
*Change pitch, tone, accent perfectly. Sound like anyone.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when impersonating someone vocally or when vocal presentation matters
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 300 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Voice glitches between identities. Compel this for breaking cover at worst times.

### ☐ Cosmetic Enhancement Package
*Perfect skin, symmetrical features, optimal attractiveness.*
- **Effect**: +1 to Cool when appearance matters (first impressions, seduction, high society)
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (maintenance stress, identity tied to appearance)
- **Maintenance**: 500 credits/month (expensive upkeep)
- **When it breaks**: Enhancement fades, looking "wrong." -1 to Cool until fixed. Identity crisis.

### ☐ Pheromone Regulator
*Subtle chemical influence. People like you without knowing why.*
- **Effect**: +1 to Cool in face-to-face social situations (doesn't work over comms)
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (manipulating people's neurochemistry feels hollow)
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Regulator floods or shuts down. Either repulse people or no effect. Compel this.

### ☐ Memory Palace Implant
*Perfect recall of conversations, names, faces, relationships.*
- **Effect**: Never forget a detail about someone. +2 to Cool when leveraging past interactions
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (weight of remembering everything)
- **Maintenance**: 350 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Memories corrupt. You confuse people, relationships, lies you told. -2 to social rolls.

### ☐ Subvocal Comm + AR Contact Lenses
*Silent communication, real-time data overlays on people you're talking to.*
- **Effect**: Access information instantly during conversations. Get fed lines, data, prompts from crew
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 250 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Can't access your lifeline. You're on your own. Take -1 to Cool.

## TALENTS (Choose 2)

### ☐ Perfect Lie (4 points)
Once per session, fabricate a complete identity with documentation, backstory, and credentials. Roll Cool—on success, it's airtight and holds up to scrutiny. On failure, it works but has one critical flaw (GM decides when it surfaces).

### ☐ Read Intent (2 points)
You see what people want before they say it. +2 to Cool when determining someone's goals, desires, or pressure points. You can ask the GM "What do they want most right now?" and get an honest answer.

### ☐ Burn Bridges (2 points)
Nuclear option. Once per session, automatically succeed at convincing someone of anything or getting them to do anything within reason. The cost: that relationship is permanently destroyed. They'll never trust you again. Choose carefully.

### ☐ Blend In (2 points)
You belong everywhere. +2 to Cool when adopting appropriate behavior for a social context (corporate exec, street vendor, high society, criminal underworld). You know the codes.

### ☐ Borrowed Authority (2 points)
When impersonating someone in authority or invoking someone else's power, +2 to Cool. You sell it convincingly. "I'm calling on behalf of Director Sato" works because you sound like you should.

### ☐ Exit Stage Left (2 points)
When a con goes bad or your cover is blown, roll Cool. On success, you have a plausible excuse or distraction that lets you leave cleanly. On failure, you get out but someone remembers your face.

## HOW TO PLAY THE FACE

### YOU'RE GOOD AT:
- Talking your way in (or out)
- Reading people instantly
- Creating believable cover stories
- Social engineering (manipulation is a skill)
- Impersonation and disguise
- Making people like you
- Finding leverage in relationships
- Being whoever the situation needs

### YOU'RE BAD AT:
- Technical work (you talk to people, not machines)
- Physical confrontation (you're not built for violence)
- Maintaining real relationships (everything feels transactional)
- Being yourself (do you even know who that is?)
- Long-term planning (you live con to con)
- Trusting people
- Stopping the performance

### WHEN YOU ROLL:
- **Body**: You probably avoid this (hire muscle)
- **Reflexes**: Getting out fast, sleight of hand, quick costume changes
- **Code**: You probably don't (hire a hacker)
- **Tech**: You probably don't (hire a rigger)
- **Cool**: Everything that matters—lying, reading people, social manipulation, impersonation, negotiation, seduction, intimidation through words

## THE FACE MOVE: WHOEVER YOU NEED ME TO BE

Once per session, when you need to become someone specific (a guard, an executive, a contact, a spouse), you can declare:

**"I've been them before" or "I know exactly how to be them."**

For one scene, you *are* that person convincingly enough that even people who know them might not notice. You have their mannerisms, knowledge, authority. You sell it perfectly.

**Limitations**:
- Doesn't give you their actual access codes or technical knowledge
- If questioned with information only the real person would know, the illusion cracks
- After the scene ends, you need to disappear—people will realize eventually

**Cost**: This takes serious identity strain. Take 2 Nerves stress after the scene ends from maintaining the performance.

## QUESTIONS TO ANSWER (Discover through play)

You don't need answers now. Find them during the game:

1. **What's your real name?** Do you even remember?
2. **Who did you betray to get where you are?** Do they know it was you?
3. **When did you realize you were better at lying than telling the truth?** How did that feel?
4. **What's the one con you regret?** Why?
5. **If you stopped performing, who would you be?** Are you afraid to find out?
6. **Who have you been that you wish you actually were?** What's stopping you?
7. **What's the difference between a good con and manipulation?** Or is there no difference?

## EXAMPLE: CASSANDRA "SILK" VALE

**High Concept**: *Former Sidereal Corporate Relations, Now Freelance Social Engineer*  
**Trouble**: *I've Played So Many Roles I Forgot Which One Is Me*  
**Connection**: *Director Sato Doesn't Know I'm the One Who Stole His Research*

**Stats**: Body +1, Reflexes +1, Code +0, Tech +0, Cool +3

**Chrome**:
- Social Analysis Suite (+1 Systems, -1 Nerves)
- Cosmetic Enhancement Package (+1 Systems, -1 Nerves)

**Final Stress**: Meat ▢▢▢ | Nerves ▢ | Systems ▢▢▢▢▢

**Talents**:
- Perfect Lie (4 points)
- Read Intent (2 points)

**Gear**: Five identity documents (ranging from corporate exec to dock worker), three burner comms, designer wardrobe, forgery kit, social calendar with 200+ contacts, 3,000 credits

**Notes**: Cassandra worked corporate relations for Meridian Holdings on Sidereal Station—the person who smoothed over problems, made executives look good, negotiated contracts with a smile. She was exceptional at it. Too good. She saw how much power came from being the person everyone confided in. She saw the leverage. One day she walked out with Director Sato's research on illegal biotech and disappeared into the system. Now she works freelance, running cons and social engineering for crews. She has twelve active identities and can't remember her original birthday. The crew calls her Silk. She's pretty sure that's not her real name. She's not sure she has a real name anymore. Sometimes she catches herself performing even when alone. That should bother her more than it does.
