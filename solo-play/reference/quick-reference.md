## THE ARTIFACTS

1. **Character Sheet** - Who Ann Sho is mechanically
2. **Relationship Map** - Who matters and why (Ron Edwards style)
3. **Fronts** - What threatens her and when (Apocalypse World style)
4. **Phase Trio** - How she connects to crew (FATE style)
5. **Solo Session** - How to play her origin story

**Each serves a different purpose. Together they create complete character.**

---

## HOW TO USE THEM

### FOR CHARACTER GENERATION

**Start with Phase Trio** (Artifact #4)
- Write Ann's origin story (Phase One) → generates HIGH CONCEPT
- Choose two crew connections (Phases Two & Three) → generates RELATIONSHIP aspects
- Define her trouble (Phase Four) → generates TROUBLE aspect
- Result: Complete aspects for character sheet

**Transfer to Character Sheet** (Artifact #1)
- Stats, stress, chrome already defined (she's an Infiltrator)
- Aspects from Phase Trio fill in personality
- Talents chosen based on how you want to play her
- Gear matches her three identities

**Build Relationship Map** (Artifact #2)
- Place Ann at center
- Add NPCs: Chen, Students, Handler, Clone, Algorithm
- Add crew connections from Phase Trio
- Draw lines showing who wants what from whom
- Map reveals drama potential

**Set Up Fronts** (Artifact #3)
- Four threats: Algorithm, Cover, Students, G0 Network
- Each has countdown clock
- Position them at starting steps (see Solo Session for initial positions)
- These create momentum and pressure

---

### FOR SOLO PREQUEL SESSION

**Use Solo Session Guide** (Artifact #5) as play structure:
- Five scenes over one evening
- Each scene tests different aspect of Ann's character
- Scenes advance fronts based on choices
- By end, character is established and ready for crew play

**Reference Character Sheet** during play:
- When rolling dice, use stats
- When taking stress, mark boxes
- When invoking aspects, spend Fate points
- When using talents, apply mechanical benefits

**Track Front Advancement** during play:
- Each scene might advance 1-2 fronts
- Mark which steps completed
- Pressure escalates naturally
- Fronts create consequences for choices

**Update Relationship Map** after play:
- Add new connections revealed
- Update who has leverage
- Note loyalty shifts
- Map shows how story changed

---

### FOR CREW PLAY

**Character Sheet** is your mechanical reference:
- Stats for rolls
- Stress for damage
- Aspects for invoking/compelling
- Talents for special moves

**Relationship Map** shows connections:
- How Ann knows other PCs (from Phase Trio)
- Which NPCs matter (Chen, Students, etc.)
- Where pressure points are
- Who can help or threaten

**Fronts** create ongoing pressure:
- GM advances them between sessions
- They don't wait for PC to act
- Create time pressure and stakes
- Force choices between priorities

**Phase Trio Connections** with crew:
- Established history with 2 crew members
- Shared experiences to reference
- Built-in trust or tension
- Story hooks for complications

## EXAMPLE: USING EVERYTHING IN ONE SESSION

### Setup (Before Play)

**Character Sheet**: Ann's stats and stress available  
**Relationship Map**: Chen, Tomas, Handler, Clone, and two crew connections mapped  
**Fronts**: Algorithm at step 5, Cover at step 3, Students at step 2, G0 at step 4  
**Phase Trio**: Established that Ann knows Nomad (extraction history) and Rigger (hardware supplier)

---

### During Play

**SCENE**: Ann needs to break into secure server room with crew

**Character Sheet**:
- Ann rolls Code +3 to bypass security
- Uses "I Know This System" talent (she audited this before)
- Success! She's in

**Relationship Map**:
- Ann calls Rigger (from Phase Trio) for hardware bypass tool
- Invokes "Rigger Builds Things The Algorithm Can't See" aspect
- +2 to roll, succeeds with style

**Fronts Advancement**:
- Breaking into server advances **Algorithm** front (it notices intrusion)
- Clean extraction prevents **Cover** advancement (no suspicion)
- But Rigger's involvement advances **G0 Network** (more people know)

**Phase Trio Connection**:
- Ann and Rigger have established trust (Phase Two crossing)
- No need to negotiate or convince
- But GM compels: "Rigger needs payment you can't afford right now"
- Ann accepts compel, takes Fate point, owes Rigger favor

---

### Result

**All tools working together**:
- Character sheet provided mechanics
- Relationship map showed who to call
- Front advancement created consequences
- Phase Trio history made interaction smooth but complicated
- Story advanced naturally

## QUICK TROUBLESHOOTING

**"I don't know what Ann would do"**  
→ Look at aspects (HIGH CONCEPT, TROUBLE)  
→ Which identity is she performing right now?  
→ What does the relationship map show about who matters?

**"This scene feels flat"**  
→ Check fronts - which one can advance?  
→ Apply pressure from relationship map  
→ Invoke or compel Phase Trio connections  
→ Force choice between identities

**"I lost track of NPCs"**  
→ Relationship map shows who exists  
→ Pick one under-used connection  
→ Have them want something from Ann  
→ Create tension

**"Fronts feel overwhelming"**  
→ Focus on one per session  
→ Advance others slowly in background  
→ Remember: PC can slow or stop fronts  
→ Pressure creates drama, not punishment

**"Phase Trio connections feel unused"**  
→ Have crew member need Ann's help  
→ Compel shared history at inconvenient time  
→ Let crew call in favors from past  
→ Build on established trust or tension

## THE PLAY PATTERN

**Every session should touch**:
1. **Character Sheet** (roll dice, use abilities)
2. **Relationship Map** (interact with someone who matters)
3. **Fronts** (at least one advances)
4. **Phase Trio** (reference crew connections)

**When all four are active**:
- Mechanics support story
- Relationships create drama
- Pressure builds naturally
- History enriches present
