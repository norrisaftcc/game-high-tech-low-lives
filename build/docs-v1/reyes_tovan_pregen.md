# REYES TOVAN
## Nomad + Solo (Smuggler)

*"The Mirasol's gotten me through worse than this. Probably."*

---

## THE LAYERS

**What The Ports See**: Independent cargo hauler. License registered to Tovan Freight LLC (single vessel operation). Runs legitimate shipping between platforms—medical supplies, replacement parts, data cores. Pays docking fees on time. Files manifests. No outstanding warrants. Boring. Reliable. Forgettable.

**What The Water Knows**: Smuggler. Knows every current between here and the Maelstrom. Knows which corp patrols run late. Knows where the surveillance nets have gaps. Runs cargo that doesn't exist on manifests. People who need to disappear book passage. The Mirasol's gotten more people out than she's gotten caught. So far.

**What You Know**: You were corporate military once. Naval interdiction for Fujikara-Pacific. Hunted people like you. Then they ordered you to sink a refugee boat. You didn't. They called it mutiny. You called it the day you stopped taking their money. The Mirasol was your patrol boat. Technically you stole her. Technically you saved 47 people and disappeared into the pelagic routes. That was eight years ago.

*The ocean is bigger than any corp. That's the only reason you're still free.*

---

## ASPECTS

**High Concept**: **Ex-Fujikara Naval Captain Gone Free Trader**

You know corporate patrol patterns because you wrote some of them. You know their communication protocols because you used them. You know where they're vulnerable because you remember being on the other side. That knowledge keeps you free. That history makes you wanted.

*Invoke*: When evading corporate patrols, predicting military response, navigating pelagic routes, using naval training against your former employers.

*Compel*: When former colleagues recognize you, when your military background conflicts with crew loyalty, when Fujikara-Pacific posts new bounties, when your patrol boat registry triggers automated flags.

**Trouble**: **The Mirasol Is Home And That Makes Her A Liability**

Your boat is everything. Transport, income, shelter, freedom. She's old—retrofit patrol vessel running on maintained-but-aging systems. Every job funds her repairs. Every repair buys another month of freedom. If she breaks down in deep water, you die. If she gets impounded, you lose everything. You take risks you shouldn't because you can't afford to lose her.

*Compel*: When repairs cost more than the job pays, when you risk the boat to save crew, when someone threatens to report her stolen registration, when mechanical failure happens at worst possible moment.

**Connection**: **Dr. Sana Kahale Patches Me Up, Asks No Questions**

Sana runs an independent med clinic on Platform Seven. Treats nomads, refugees, people without corporate health plans. She's patched you up more times than you can count. Never reported the gunshot wounds. Never asked why you show up at 3 AM bleeding. You owe her. She knows it. She hasn't collected yet.

*Invoke*: When you need medical care off-grid, when someone in your network needs help, when Sana's reputation gets you trust with refugees or nomads.

*Compel*: When Sana finally calls in the debt, when her clinic gets threatened because she treated you, when you have to choose between her safety and a job.

## CHROME & AUGMENTATION

**Pressure Adaptation Mods** (Cardiovascular Enhancement, Military Standard)
- Enhanced oxygen processing, pressure tolerance for deep diving
- *Effect*: Ignore environmental penalties from water pressure or low oxygen. Can dive deeper and longer than baseline humans.
- *Origin*: Fujikara-Pacific naval issue. Permanent installation. Can't be removed without serious surgery.
- *Cost*: +1 Systems capacity, -1 Meat capacity
- *Maintenance*: 200 credits/month (you do it yourself when possible)

**Neural Link (Boat Interface)** (Cortical Port, Custom Installation)
- Direct connection to the Mirasol's systems. Fly her like an extension of your body.
- *Effect*: +2 to Reflexes when piloting the Mirasol specifically. Feel her systems like proprioception.
- *Limitation*: Only works with your boat. Installing it in another vessel requires reprogramming that takes weeks.
- *Cost*: +1 Systems capacity
- *Maintenance*: Minimal (connected to boat, not external network)

**Prosthetic Left Hand** (Mechanical Replacement, Field Installation)
- Lost the original to patrol boat explosion. This one is functional, not pretty.
- *Effect*: Slightly stronger grip than flesh. Magnetic palm for securing tools. Makes poor life choices visible to anyone who looks.
- *Story*: The day you took the Mirasol. Emergency bulkhead crushed it during escape. Sana installed this one.
- *Cost*: +1 Systems capacity, -1 Meat capacity
- *Maintenance*: You repair it yourself. Parts are scavenged.

**The Mirasol Knows The Routes** (4 points)
- Eight years running cargo. You know every current, every checkpoint, every gap in patrol coverage.
- *Effect*: When piloting the Mirasol through pelagic routes, automatically succeed at navigation and evasion once per session. You've done this run a hundred times. You'll do it a hundred more.

**Contacts Everywhere** (2 points)
- Every port has someone who owes you or someone you've helped. Refugees you smuggled. Nomads you traded with. People who remember favors.

**Combat Veteran** (2 points)
- Fujikara-Pacific trained you to fight. You didn't forget when you left.
- *Effect*: +2 to Body or Reflexes during combat extended contests. You've been shot at by professionals. You survived by being better.

## WHO YOU ARE (Choose Your Truth)

**The Deserter**: You broke your oath. Stole military property. Endangered personnel during escape. Fujikara-Pacific called it mutiny. They're right. You'd do it again. Some orders are illegal to follow.

**The Pragmatist**: You made a business decision. Naval pay wasn't worth murdering refugees. Taking the boat was severance package. Running cargo pays better anyway. The freedom is bonus. You don't think about the 47 people you saved. They're not your problem anymore.

**The Believer**: The ocean is bigger than any corp. The Mirasol was built to patrol it, but she was meant to be free. You didn't steal her—you liberated her. Every person you smuggle, every corp blockade you slip through, every day you stay free is proof the system has cracks. You're living in one.

*The truth is what you tell yourself at 3 AM when the guilt or the pride keeps you awake.*

## RELATIONSHIPS & LEVERAGE

**Dr. Sana Kahale** (Platform Seven Clinic): Knows about the gunshot wounds, the burns, the concussion from that job that went wrong. Never reported you. Never asked for payment beyond "stay alive, you fool." You owe her your life several times over. She will collect eventually.

**Fujikara-Pacific Naval Command** (Former Employer): Wants you arrested. Wants the Mirasol returned. Wants example made. Eight-year-old warrant still active. Bounty increased twice. You're not important enough to actively hunt, but any patrol that spots you gets promoted for bringing you in.

**The Nomad Network** (Informal Community): Knows you run clean. Knows you don't rat. Knows you got people out when they needed out. That reputation is why refugees still contact you. That reputation is why you can't say no.

**The Crew** (Variable): Whoever you hire for this job. They're not permanent. They're professionals. You pay fair, they work clean, everyone parts ways. The Mirasol is yours. Crew is temporary. That's the rule. That's been the rule for eight years. That's definitely going to stay the rule. (You tell yourself this repeatedly.)

## THE MIRASOL

She's a Mark-VII Coastal Interdiction Vessel. Built for Fujikara-Pacific Naval Division. Hull number FP-7283. Decommissioned eight years ago under "irregular circumstances." (The circumstances: you stole her.)

She's 42 meters long. Displaces 85 tons. Twin turbine engines that sound like angry gods when pushed. Reinforced hull that's taken hits from patrol boats, corp security, and once from a very angry whale.

She's got hidden cargo compartments that weren't in the original design. You added those. She's got a forward cannon that's "disabled" according to your registry. It works fine. She's got a coffee maker in the galley that's held together with spit and prayers. You replace the coffee maker, everything else is negotiable.

She leaks when pushed hard. The starboard engine runs hot. The nav computer has opinions about your piloting. The hull creaks in ways that mean something if you speak her language.

She's gotten you out of everything so far.

*She's home.*
