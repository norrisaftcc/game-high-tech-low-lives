# Player's Guide

## The Core Roll

Roll four Fate dice. Each shows plus (+1), blank (0), or minus (-1). Add your relevant stat. Compare to difficulty or opposing roll.

**Your Stats** range from +0 (competent) to +3 (exceptional):

- **Body**: Physical endurance, strength, resilience
- **Reflexes**: Speed, precision, reaction time
- **Code**: Digital intrusion, programming, system manipulation
- **Tech**: Hardware engineering, repair, jury-rigging
- **Cool**: Nerve, presence, emotional control

## Stress and Breaking Points

You have three stress tracks. Each represents a different way you can break:

- **Meat**: Physical damage. Bullets, falls, toxins.
- **Nerves**: Psychological strain. Fear, pressure, betrayal.
- **Systems**: Technical damage to chrome and gear.

When you get hit, mark stress boxes equal to the shifts. If your track is full, take a Consequence or you're taken out.

**Consequences** last:
- **Mild** (2 shifts): Clears after one scene. *Bruised Ribs*, *Rattled*
- **Serious** (4 shifts): Requires attention. *Compound Fracture*, *Chrome Glitching*
- **Severe** (6 shifts): Permanent until bought off. *Neural Scarring*, *Lost an Eye*

## Fate Points

Start each session with 3 Fate Points. Spend them to:

**Invoke an Aspect** (+2 to your roll or reroll): Use something true about you, the situation, or your opponent. Costs 1 FP.

**Power a Talent**: Some talents cost FP to activate. You declare, you pay, it happens.

**Refuse Being Taken Out**: Stay conscious when you should drop. Costs 1 FP. Take an additional consequence.

Gain FP when your Trouble aspect causes you problems, or when you accept a compel that makes your life harder.

## Talents

You start with **3 talents**. Each is a special capability that breaks the rules in a specific way.

**Example Talents:**

*Combat Reflexes*: Once per scene, act first regardless of circumstances.

*Chrome Integration* (Passive): Add +1 stress box to your Systems track.

*Neural Firewall*: Spend 1 FP to automatically succeed at defending against Code attacks.

*Pressure Adapted* (Passive): Ignore environmental complications from depth or vacuum.

*Fast Talk*: Spend 1 FP to create a situation aspect with a free invoke when you lie convincingly.

*Emergency Protocols*: Spend 1 FP to clear all Meat stress boxes. Once per session.

## Conflicts in Play

### Single Roll Conflicts

State stakes before rolling. What happens if you win? What happens if you lose? Both sides agree.

Roll once. Highest total wins. Stakes happen immediately. No negotiation after dice fall.

**Ties**: Split the outcome or success with cost.

### Extended Contests

Use when one roll isn't enough—chases, firefights, hacking through ice.

Set victory condition: first to three successes, first to inflict 4 stress, extract data before lockdown.

Roll each exchange. Track successes. When condition is met, stakes resolve completely.

## Example: Deep Runner from Palliatives

**Karo**, heavily chromed salvage specialist working medical waste routes

**Stats**: Body +2, Reflexes +2, Code +0, Tech +3, Cool +1

**Stress**: Meat 2, Nerves 3, Systems 5 (chrome-heavy)

**Talents**:
- *Rebreather Implant* (Passive): Survive one hour underwater without gear
- *Pressure Adapted* (Passive): No depth penalties, ignore pressure complications
- *Salvage Eye*: Spend 1 FP to automatically locate valuable components in wreckage

**Aspects**:
- High Concept: *Palliatives Salvage Runner Who Knows the Deep Routes*
- Trouble: *The Chrome Made Me Different*

### Scene: Recovering Medical Supplies from Sunk Facility

**The Setup**: Palliatives medical district needs expensive diagnostic equipment. Karo knows where some sank when the old research tower flooded—forty meters down, unstable structure.

**GM**: "The tower is partially collapsed. You can see the equipment through a cracked viewport, but the corridor is flooded and debris blocks the way. The structure groans. It could shift."

**Karo's player**: "I check the structural integrity first. Can I reach it safely?"

**GM**: "That's a Tech roll, difficulty +3. High because the building could collapse."

**Roll**: Karo rolls 4dF and gets [0, +1, -1, +1] = +1. Add Tech +3 = total +4. Success!

**GM**: "You identify the safe approach—through the ventilation system, not the main corridor. No time pressure if you're careful."

**Karo's player**: "I swim through. My rebreather handles the time underwater."

**GM**: "You reach the room. The equipment is intact but the structural groaning is getting worse. You need to work fast. That's an extended contest against the collapsing building. First to two successes wins."

**Exchange 1 - Reflexes vs. difficulty +2**:
- Roll: [-1, 0, -1, +1] = -1, +2 Reflexes = +1 total
- Difficulty: +2
- **Building wins this exchange**. "A support beam cracks. Water pressure increases. Mark 1 Meat stress as debris clips your shoulder."

**Exchange 2 - Tech vs. difficulty +2**:
- Roll: [+1, +1, 0, +1] = +3, +3 Tech = +6 total
- Karo's player: "I invoke my *Palliatives Salvage Runner* aspect—I've done this kind of extraction dozens of times. Spending 1 FP for +2."
- Total: +8 vs. +2
- **Karo succeeds by 6 shifts!** That's two successes.

**GM**: "You tear through the securing mounts, bundle the equipment, and kick off the wall just as the ceiling caves in. You're clear, equipment secured. The medical district will be grateful."

**Result**: Karo succeeds but took 1 Meat stress and spent 1 FP. The diagnostic equipment is recovered.

## Example: Station Tech from Sidereal

**Wen**, orbital maintenance tech with corporate access

**Stats**: Body +1, Reflexes +1, Code +2, Tech +3, Cool +1

**Stress**: Meat 3, Nerves 3, Systems 3 (balanced, minimal chrome)

**Talents**:
- *Corporate Credentials* (Passive): Access to restricted areas without suspicion
- *System Backdoor*: Spend 1 FP to automatically find hidden vulnerabilities in station systems
- *Zero-G Trained* (Passive): Ignore complications from microgravity

**Aspects**:
- High Concept: *Sidereal Maintenance Tech Who Knows Every System*
- Trouble: *What I Know Could Get Me Killed*

### Scene: Stealing Data from Secure Archive

**The Setup**: Wen needs to access classified research data stored in Sidereal's isolated data core. The system is air-gapped. Physical access required. Security is professional.

**GM**: "You're on shift. The data core is three levels down from your current section. There's one security checkpoint between you and access."

**Wen's player**: "I walk there like I belong. My credentials should get me through."

**GM**: "That's Cool, difficulty +2. The guard is bored but professional."

**Roll**: [+1, 0, 0, -1] = 0, +1 Cool = +1 total vs. +2. **Failure**.

**GM**: "The guard stops you. 'What's your business in Data Core 7?' He's checking his terminal. Your credentials show you're cleared for maintenance access, but there's no work order filed."

**Wen's player**: "I think fast. 'Environmental sensor fault. I need to check the air handling system before it cascades.'"

**GM**: "That's a Cool roll to fast-talk, opposed by his Cool +1."

**Wen's player**: "I'll use my *System Backdoor* talent. I spend 1 FP and pull up a fake sensor alert on my tablet—actually happens through system vulnerability. Makes my story believable."

**GM**: "That's convincing evidence. He doesn't even need to roll. 'Make it quick. Log your time.'"

**Inside the data core**:

**GM**: "The system is isolated. You need physical access to copy the data. The terminal requires biometric authentication plus a rotating security key. That's Code, difficulty +4."

**Roll**: [+1, +1, -1, 0] = +1, +2 Code = +3 total vs. +4. **Failure by 1**.

**Wen's player**: "I invoke my *Sidereal Tech Who Knows Every System* aspect. I helped install this security. 1 FP for +2."

**Total**: +5 vs. +4. **Success!**

**GM**: "You're in. The data downloads to your concealed storage. You've got about forty seconds before the access is logged and someone investigates."

**Wen's player**: "I'm already moving. Back to my assigned section, file a fake maintenance report, disappear into shift-change."

**Result**: Wen succeeded but spent 2 FP. The data is secured. Security might notice the discrepancy later, but Wen is clear for now.

## The Shared Imagined Space

When you describe your actions, **describe what happens in the world**, not just game mechanics.

**Don't say**: "I want to use Reflexes to dodge."

**Do say**: "The beam is falling—I twist sideways and push off the bulkhead to get clear."

When you invoke an aspect, **explain how it helps**:

**Don't say**: "I invoke my High Concept for +2."

**Do say**: "I've navigated these sunken routes for years—I know exactly where the current pulls strong. That's my *Palliatives Salvage Runner* experience."

When you take stress or consequences, **describe the harm**:

**Don't say**: "I mark 3 Meat stress."

**Do say**: "The impact slams me into the bulkhead. I feel ribs crack. I'm hurt but still moving."

The mechanics track what happens. The description creates what we all imagine together. Both matter.

## Advancement

After each session, you may advance one element:

- **Increase a stat** from +0 to +1, or +1 to +2 (costs 2 advances to go from +2 to +3)
- **Gain a new talent**
- **Buy off a Severe consequence** (becomes a permanent aspect instead)
- **Swap a talent** for a different one that makes sense for your character
- **Add +1 stress box** to one track (maximum twice per track)

You grow slowly. Every advance represents meaningful change.

## The Key Principle

When the dice stop rolling, what was decided happens. No hedging. No "yes but" unless that was the stated stakes.

You declare. You roll. Fiction changes.

Everything costs. Chrome shifts your stress tracks. Talents require FP. Consequences reshape you. You gain capability through sacrifice.

That's the premise.
