# HIGH-TECH-LOW-LIVES
## THE FIXER

*You know people. That's your power. Everyone owes someone, and you know who owes what. You're the broker, the connector, the person who makes introductions and opens doors. You don't hack systems or fight—you call someone who can. Your network is your weapon. The question isn't whether you can solve the problem—it's whether you're willing to spend the favor.*

## CHROME (Choose 2)

You use chrome to enhance your network and remember connections.

### ☐ Social Analysis Suite
*Read micro-expressions, vocal stress, pupil dilation. Know what people really want.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when reading people's emotions, detecting lies, or assessing what they need
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (everyone is transparent, you see all their needs)
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: You're reading people wrong, missing crucial cues. -2 to social rolls.

### ☐ Network Database Implant
*Every contact, every favor, every debt—instantly accessible.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when recalling who knows what, who owes whom, or how people are connected. Never forget a contact.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (your network never lets you rest)
- **Maintenance**: 450 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Database corrupted. You lose track of favors, debts, connections. Dangerous.

### ☐ Enhanced Negotiation Package
*Vocal modulation, pheromone regulation, subtle influence tech.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when negotiating deals, brokering agreements, or mediating conflicts
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (constant manipulation takes its toll)
- **Maintenance**: 500 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: You lose your edge in negotiations. -2 to Cool until fixed.

### ☐ Favor Tracker Implant
*Track debts, promises, and obligations in real-time. Know who owes what.*
- **Effect**: Once per session, declare someone owes you a favor (GM approves nature/scope). +1 to Cool when calling in debts.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 300 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Can't remember who owes you. Someone might refuse a legitimate debt.

### ☐ Multi-Comm Hub
*Manage multiple simultaneous conversations across different channels.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Tech when coordinating specialists, arranging meetings, or managing complex operations. Communicate with entire network simultaneously.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (constant connectivity, no downtime)
- **Maintenance**: 350 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Limited to one conversation at a time. Your network coordination suffers.

### ☐ Subvocal Comm + AR Contact Lenses

- **Effect**: Access your network's information instantly during conversations. Get fed intel, connections, data from specialists.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 250 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Cut off from your network during conversations. -1 to Cool.

## TALENTS (Choose 2)

### ☐ Network of Specialists (4 points)
You know experts in every field. Once per session, declare you know someone who can handle a specific technical problem (hacker, medic, pilot, forger, whatever). Roll Cool—on success, they're available and owe you a favor. On failure, they can help but they need something from you first. You maintain relationships with 6-12 specialists.

### ☐ Read Intent (2 points)
You see what people want before they say it. +2 to Cool when determining someone's goals, desires, or pressure points. You can ask the GM "What do they want most right now?" and get an honest answer.

### ☐ Everyone Owes Someone (2 points)
You track debts and favors systematically. Once per session, declare someone owes you (GM approves scope). They have to pay up or explain why they can't. Alternatively, take 2 Nerves stress to make them do it regardless—but relationships have consequences.

### ☐ Neutral Ground (2 points)
You establish and maintain safe meeting places. Once per session, declare a location where violence is prohibited by mutual agreement. If anyone breaks the peace, they're cut off from the network. You're the one who enforces neutrality.

### ☐ Broker's Ear (2 points)
You hear things. +2 to Cool when gathering information through your network. You know who's hiring, who's looking, who's in trouble. Information flows through you. Once per session, the GM tells you about an opportunity before anyone else knows.

### ☐ Mediate (2 points)
You resolve conflicts between parties. +2 to Cool when negotiating between opposed groups or mediating disputes. Both sides trust you to be fair—or at least predictable. You make deals happen.

- **Code**: Basic understanding, enough to know what you're brokering
- **Tech**: Understanding technical problems so you can find the right specialist
- **Cool**: Your primary skill—negotiating, reading people, maintaining relationships, brokering deals, calling in favors

## THE FIXER MOVE: I KNOW SOMEONE WHO CAN HELP

Once per session, when the crew faces a problem that seems impossible, you can declare:

**"I know someone who can handle this."**

You introduce a specialist from your network who has exactly the expertise needed. This person exists, they're available (more or less), and they're capable. You vouch for them.

**What you get**:
- A competent specialist (hacker, pilot, medic, forger, muscle, whatever)
- They're willing to help (though they might want payment)
- They can solve this specific problem
- The GM defines who they are and what they're like

**What it costs**:
- This specialist now expects you to vouch for the crew
- If the crew burns them, your reputation suffers
- They'll remember this favor—you might owe them later
- Your network isn't infinite (use carefully)

**Limitations**:
- They help with THIS problem, not permanent crew
- They have their own lives and limitations
- If you abuse this, your network stops answering

## EXAMPLE: MARCUS "WIRE" CHEN

**High Concept**: *Sidereal Station Broker with Connections in Every Sector*
**Trouble**: *Meridian Holdings and Nexus Corp Both Think I'm Their Exclusive Contact*
**Connection**: *Yana Reyes Saved My Network When It Almost Collapsed*

**Stats**: Body +1, Reflexes +2, Code +1, Tech +2, Cool +3

**Chrome**:
- Network Database Implant (+1 Systems, -1 Nerves)
- Social Analysis Suite (+1 Systems, -1 Nerves)

**Final Stress**: Meat ▢▢▢ | Nerves ▢ | Systems ▢▢▢▢▢

**Talents**:
- Network of Specialists (4 points)
- Everyone Owes Someone (2 points)

**Gear**: Contact database (73 active connections across 5 sectors), four comm devices (different networks), professional wardrobe, favor ledger (meticulously maintained), access to three neutral meeting spaces, 3,500 credits

**Notes**: Marcus started as a junior logistics coordinator for Sidereal Station operations—the person who scheduled maintenance, coordinated vendors, arranged meetings. Not glamorous. But it meant he knew everyone who made the station actually work. When a strike threatened to shut down oxygen recycling, Marcus brokered the deal that kept everyone breathing. After that, people remembered him. He started connecting people who needed each other—a medic who needed parts with an engineer who needed medical care. A pilot who needed passengers with refugees who needed transport. Five years later, he's the person you call when you need something impossible. His network spans corporate executives, dock workers, smugglers, and station administration. He maintains strict neutrality—his meeting spaces are safe zones where violence is absolutely forbidden. Break that rule and you're blacklisted permanently. Marcus has two problems: Meridian Holdings pays him a monthly retainer thinking he's their exclusive intelligence source. Nexus Corp pays him a different retainer thinking the same thing. Eventually, they'll figure it out. He's building leverage to survive that day. The crew calls him Wire because he's always connected. He has 73 active contacts and remembers every favor owed and every debt unpaid. His network is his life. Losing it would destroy him.
