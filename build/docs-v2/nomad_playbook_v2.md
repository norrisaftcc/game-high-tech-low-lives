# HIGH-TECH-LOW-LIVES
## THE NOMAD

*You're a captain, pilot, driver—someone who moves between places. Your vehicle is your home, your freedom, your livelihood. You know the routes others don't, the ways around corporate checkpoints, the safe harbors. You take people where they need to go. Sometimes that's dangerous. Sometimes that's all that matters.*

## YOUR BOAT

**Your vehicle is a character.** Work with the GM to define:
- **Name**: What do you call it?
- **Type**: Boat, shuttle, surface transport, cargo hauler
- **Personality**: Reliable but cranky, fast but fragile, tough but slow
- **Signature Feature**: One thing it does exceptionally well

**Boat Stress Track**: Systems ▢▢▢▢▢▢ (separate from your personal Systems)

When your boat takes damage, mark boat stress. When boat stress fills, it takes consequences like a character (engine damage, hull breach, navigation failure). You can clear boat stress through repair work (Tech rolls, time, credits for parts).

## CHROME (Choose 2)

You chrome for piloting and survival, not combat or hacking.

### ☐ Pilot Interface
*Direct neural connection to your vehicle. You don't pilot it—you ARE it.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Reflexes when piloting your boat
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (disconnection from body while jacked in)
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Manual control only. -2 to piloting until fixed. Feels like being blind.

### ☐ Environmental Adaptation
*Enhanced lungs, radiation filtering, toxin resistance.*
- **Effect**: +2 Body against environmental hazards (toxins, radiation, pressure changes)
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 300 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Environment hits harder. No protection until repaired.

### ☐ Navigation Suite
*Enhanced spatial awareness, route calculation, hazard prediction.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Reflexes for navigation, avoiding hazards, finding safe routes
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 350 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Navigation by instinct only. -1 to route finding.

### ☐ Emergency Overload
*Push your body past limits temporarily.*
- **Effect**: Once per session, ignore all Meat stress for one scene. Afterward, take Mild Consequence: *Exhausted*
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Meat (chronic strain)
- **Maintenance**: 450 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Can't push through anymore. You feel every injury immediately.

### ☐ Cargo Scanner
*Built-in sensors. Know what you're carrying.*
- **Effect**: Automatically detect contraband, weapons, explosives in cargo. +2 Tech scanning.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 250 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Blind loading. You don't know what you're carrying until someone tells you.

### ☐ Combat Reflexes
*Ex-military augmentation. Still works.*
- **Effect**: +1 to Reflexes in combat situations
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Delayed responses. -1 to combat reflexes.

**After choosing chrome, calculate your final stress tracks.**

## TALENTS (Choose 2)

### ☐ Know the Routes (4 points)
Once per session, declare you know a route that gets the crew where they need to go. It might be dangerous, it might be slow, but it exists and you know it. No roll required. GM tells you the risk.

### ☐ Free Captain (2 points)
You take orders from no one. +2 to Cool when resisting authority, refusing unjust commands, or maintaining independence. Corps and governments know you can't be bought.

### ☐ People Smuggling (2 points)
You transport people who need to disappear. Once per session, introduce passengers who need help. They're desperate, probably have enemies, definitely complicate things. But you don't turn them away. GM determines who they are and what trouble follows.

### ☐ Emergency Repairs (2 points)
+2 to Tech for field repairs on your boat. You can keep it running with duct tape and prayers. Repairs made this way hold until the job's done, then need proper fixing.

### ☐ Combat Pilot (2 points)
+2 to Reflexes when piloting in combat or evasion situations. You've been shot at before. You know how to not get hit.

### ☐ Safe Harbor (2 points)
You know places off the grid—stations, harbors, locations where people can hide or resupply. Once per session, declare you know somewhere safe nearby. GM determines what "safe" means and what it costs to use.

## HOW TO PLAY THE NOMAD

### YOU'RE GOOD AT:
- Piloting under any conditions
- Navigation through dangerous territory
- Keeping your boat running
- Evasion and escape
- Combat piloting when necessary
- Reading weather/space hazards
- Finding safe routes and safe harbors
- Saying yes to people who need transport

### YOU'RE BAD AT:
- Staying in one place
- Following orders from authorities
- Trusting corporations
- Saying no to refugees and desperate people
- Maintaining your boat properly (always need repairs)
- Social situations (you're better with machines)
- Walking away when someone needs help

### WHEN YOU ROLL:
- **Body**: Endurance, surviving harsh environments, manual labor
- **Reflexes**: Piloting, evasion, combat, quick reactions
- **Code**: You probably don't (hire a hacker)
- **Tech**: Repairing your boat, understanding systems, jury-rigging
- **Cool**: Resisting authority, staying calm under pressure, intimidation

## THE NOMAD MOVE: NOBODY STOPS ME

Once per session, when authority tries to stop you—checkpoint, blockade, customs, security—you can declare:

**"Nobody stops me."**

You're getting through. Roll Reflexes:
- **Success**: You're past them cleanly. They can't pursue or don't know which way you went.
- **Failure**: You're through, but they know who you are, or your boat takes damage, or you had to leave something/someone behind.

This works once because they underestimate you, don't expect the route you take, or can't follow where you go. After that, they know you're serious.

Your boat might suffer (mark boat stress), but you get where you're going.

## QUESTIONS TO ANSWER (Discover through play)

You don't need answers now. Find them during the game:

1. **How did you get your boat?** Did you buy it, steal it, inherit it?
2. **What's the most dangerous route you know?** Have you survived it?
3. **Who did you fail to save?** Do you still think about them?
4. **Why did you leave military/corporate/previous life?** Can you go back?
5. **What passenger changed your life?** Where did you take them?
6. **If your boat was destroyed, what would you do?** Could you be something other than a captain?
7. **What's the one place you return to?** Why?

## EXAMPLE: REYES TOVAN

**High Concept**: *Ex-Military Boat Captain Who Smuggles Golgotha Refugees*  
**Trouble**: *Can't Say No to People Desperate Enough to Ask*  
**Connection**: *My Boat "Dust Runner" Is the Only Home I've Ever Had*

**Stats**: Body +2, Reflexes +3, Code +0, Tech +3, Cool +1

**Chrome**:
- Pilot Interface (+1 Systems, -1 Nerves)
- Environmental Adaptation (+1 Systems)

**Final Stress**: Meat ▢▢▢ | Nerves ▢▢ | Systems ▢▢▢▢▢

**Boat**: "Dust Runner" - Systems ▢▢▢▢▢▢

**Talents**:
- Know the Routes (4 points)
- People Smuggling (2 points)

**Gear**: Dust Runner (surface transport rated for Golgotha toxic atmosphere), navigation charts, environmental suits (for passengers), repair tools, sidearm, 1,500 credits

**Notes**: Reyes flew military transports for Sidereal Armed Forces for eight years. They were good at it. Too good. Command started using them for operations they didn't want logged—transporting prisoners to black sites, moving equipment for unauthorized programs. Reyes flew where ordered. Then they were ordered to transport Golgotha miners to "processing"—which meant corporate prison for trying to unionize. Reyes flew them to the nearest independent station instead, stole the transport, disappeared into the dust storms. Now they run the Dust Runner, taking jobs and smuggling refugees when they can. The boat's falling apart. Maintenance costs more than they make. But every family they get off Golgotha makes it worth it. Sidereal military still wants them back. Or dead. Either one works for them. Reyes knows every storm pattern, every radiation zone, every route the corps don't monitor. Nobody stops them. Not when people need transport. Not when lives depend on making the run.

## NARRATIVE AUTHORITY: WHAT YOU CAN DECLARE

As the Nomad, you have explicit permission to introduce these elements into the shared fiction:

**Routes**: "I know a way that gets us there"  
**Safe Harbors**: "I know a place we can hide/resupply"  
**Passengers**: "Someone needs transport—they're desperate"  
**Your Boat**: Treat it as a character with agency  
**Navigation**: "Here's the risk of this route"

You don't ask permission to move—you declare how you're getting everyone where they need to go, and deal with consequences.
