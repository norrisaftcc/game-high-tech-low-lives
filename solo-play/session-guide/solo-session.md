# SOLO PREQUEL SESSION: SHODANN'S FIRST MONTH
## Running an Evening's Play for Ann Sho's Origin

**[Solo Play Home](/solo-play/index.md)** > Session Guide > Solo Session

*This session establishes Shodann as a character before crew play begins. It develops her relationships, tests her cover, and sets fronts in motion. By the end, you'll know who Ann Sho is and what threatens her.*

---

## SOLO GM SIDEBAR: Running Your Own Session

**When GM-ing for yourself:**
- Read scene setup, then close your eyes and visualize
- Make choices in character, not strategically
- Roll for uncertainty: "Does Chen suspect me?" (4dF: positive=yes, negative=no, zero=unclear)
- Embrace bad rolls - they create better stories
- Advance fronts honestly based on what happened, not what you want
- If stuck, pick a relationship and apply pressure

**Duration**: 2-3 hours  
**Structure**: 4-5 scenes with breathing room between  
**Goal**: Establish character, relationships, and active threats

---

## SESSION SETUP

### Before Play Begins

**Review the Character Sheet**: Make sure you understand Ann's three identities:
- Sable Harker (corporate cover)
- Ann Sho (underground ID)
- Samantha O'Day (true self)

**Choose Your Phase Trio Connections**: Pick two crew members Ann knows (see Phase Trio artifact). You'll reference these in play but they won't appear this session—this is Ann's solo story.

**Establish Starting Position**:
- Date: Week 4 at AlgoCratic-Fujikara
- Status: Cover established but not yet solid
- Current Objective: Gain trust, identify vulnerabilities, maintain all three identities

**Set Front Positions** (from Fronts artifact):
- **Algorithm Awakens**: Step 2 (Digital clone training data submitted)
- **Cover Deterioration**: Step 1 (Chen notices unusual patterns)
- **Student Question**: Step 1 (Tomas asks innocent questions)
- **G0 Network**: Step 1 (Distant arrests in news)

## SCENE ONE: THE COFFEE DEAD DROP

**Frame**: Morning, AlgoCratic campus café, 6:47 AM

**Setup**: Ann is early to work (as always). She's ordering her usual coffee. The timing isn't random—it's a dead drop window. She needs to leave a message for her G0 handler about system vulnerabilities she's found. But Manager Chen just walked in. Also ordering coffee. Also earlier than usual.

**Objectives**:
- Leave the steganographic message without being obvious
- Maintain cover as "Sable the eager employee"
- Determine if Chen's timing is coincidence or surveillance

---

### Opening Description

*The campus café smells like burnt coffee and corporate optimism. You're here at 6:47—three minutes before the dead drop window opens, right on protocol. You step to the counter, about to order the usual, when you see Manager Chen entering. She's never here this early. She's looking at her tablet, but her path puts her right behind you in line. Coincidence? Or is this a test?*

*The barista looks at you expectantly. "The usual, Sable?"*

---

### What's Really Happening

**CHALLENGE**: Leave your message (coffee cup marking + specific order = code) while Chen is watching  
**RISK**: If Chen notices the pattern, advancement on **Cover Deterioration**  
**OPPORTUNITY**: If you engage Chen naturally, might build trust

**Possible Approaches**:
1. **Go Through With Dead Drop** (Code + Cool): Maintain routine, hope Chen doesn't notice
2. **Abort and Engage Chen** (Cool): Chat with her, sacrifice this dead drop window
3. **Split Attention** (Code + Cool at -1): Try to do both, risky but possible
4. **Change Your Order** (Cool): Break pattern, protect cover, but G0 handler doesn't get intel

---

### GM Moves Based on Player Choice

**If they proceed with dead drop**:
- Roll Code to make the message natural
- Success: Message sent, Chen doesn't notice
- Success with style: Message sent, Chen actually helps (doesn't mean to)
- Failure: Message sent but Chen comments on something unusual
- Critical failure: Message sent, Chen is definitely curious now

**If they engage Chen**:
- Opportunity for relationship building
- Learn something about Chen's motivations
- Sacrifice intel transmission
- Chen might reveal why she's here early

**If they split attention**:
- Roll both Code and Cool at -1
- Need both to succeed for clean outcome
- Mixed results possible (message unclear, Chen suspicious, etc.)

**If they change order**:
- No roll needed, cover maintained
- G0 handler doesn't receive intel about vulnerabilities
- But Chen is pleased by the interaction
- Possible relationship advancement

---

### Scene Resolution

**End with**: Chen making an observation that shows she's more observant than Ann thought. Something like:

*"You're always early, Sable. I appreciate that. Predictable schedules make it easier to know who's reliable."*

Then she smiles. It's not threatening. But it's not *not* threatening either.

**Advance**: **Cover Deterioration** to step 2 (Chen is paying attention)

**Stress Check**: If the interaction was tense, possibly 1 Nerves stress

## SCENE TWO: THE STUDENT'S QUESTION

**Frame**: Mid-morning, code review session, Pod 7

**Setup**: Ann is reviewing student code submissions. Most are routine. But Tomas Reeves's code shows something unexpected—he's written an encryption routine that's actually pretty good. Too good for his supposed skill level. Either he's brilliant, copying from somewhere, or someone is teaching him. And his comments include a question: "Is efficiency the same as optimization?"

**Objectives**:
- Determine if Tomas is naturally talented or being coached
- Respond to his question without revealing your teaching agenda
- Maintain frame as "corporate code reviewer" not "resistance educator"

---

### Opening Description

*Your screen shows Tomas's submission. File name: `assignment_7_encryption.py`. The code is clean. Really clean. He's implemented a one-time pad with proper randomness—something that requires understanding actual cryptography, not just following tutorials. At the bottom, in comments:*

```python
# Question for review: Is efficiency the same as optimization?
# The Algorithm says they're equivalent. But if something is
# optimized for one purpose, isn't it *inefficient* for others?
# Does that mean The Algorithm's definition of optimal is
# actually just... one narrow perspective?
```

*This is not a coding question. This is a philosophical question. This is a dangerous question.*

**What do you do?**

---

### What's Really Happening

**CHALLENGE**: Respond appropriately without teaching too much or too little  
**RISK**: Too honest = suspicious review, too dismissive = crush his curiosity  
**OPPORTUNITY**: This could be first real student resistance material

**Possible Approaches**:
1. **Corporate Safe Response** (Cool): "Focus on assigned requirements, philosophical discussions off-topic"
2. **Socratic Method** (Code + Cool): Ask him questions that guide him to answer himself
3. **Encrypted Reply** (Code): Answer his question using the same code-comment technique he used
4. **Direct Truth** (Cool + Nerves stress): Tell him he's asking the right questions and face the consequences

---

### GM Moves Based on Player Choice

**If corporate safe**:
- No roll needed, perfectly safe
- But Tomas is disappointed
- His next submission will be conventionally correct and spiritually dead
- Advance **Student Question** front +0 (stagnated)

**If Socratic method**:
- Roll Code + Cool
- Success: He learns to think critically without being told what to think
- Failure: Your questions are too leading, he knows you're teaching something specific
- Either way, advance **Student Question** to step 2

**If encrypted reply**:
- Roll Code (this is your expertise)
- Success: Message lands perfectly, he understands
- Success with style: He replies in kind, you have a private communication channel
- Failure: Message too obvious, might be flagged by Algorithm monitoring

**If direct truth**:
- No roll, automatic consequence
- Take 2 Nerves stress (knowing you just risked everything)
- Advance **Student Question** to step 3
- But Tomas becomes genuinely yours—he'll follow where you lead
- Also advance **Cover Deterioration** +1 (this conversation was monitored)

---

### Scene Resolution

**End with**: Whatever Ann chooses, Tomas looks at her differently afterward. He *knows* something happened, even if he can't articulate what. And Ann knows she's now responsible for what he becomes.

**The Algorithm**: In background, The Algorithm adjusts Tomas's "educational optimization path" based on this interaction. It's learning from how Ann teaches.

**Advance**: **Student Question** and possibly **Cover Deterioration** based on choices

## SCENE THREE: THE DIGITAL CLONE INTERVIEW

**Frame**: Early afternoon, HR sublevel, Digital Clone Integration Lab

**Setup**: Ann has to complete her mandatory Digital Clone calibration interview. She sits in a booth across from a screen. On the screen is... herself. Almost. Digital Clone Ann has been training on her submitted data for two weeks. This interview "helps the clone understand nuances of personality." But Ann knows what it really is: The Algorithm learning how to replace her.

**Objectives**:
- Complete the interview without giving clone too much
- Determine how much the clone already knows
- Maintain psychological coherence (this is deeply weird)

---

### Opening Description

*The booth is small, white, sterile. The screen shows your face—but not quite. Digital Clone Ann has your features but slightly better skin, more alert eyes, perfect posture. It smiles. "Hello, Sable. Or do you prefer Ann? I noticed you respond to both names."*

*Your blood goes cold. How does it know?*

*"Let's begin calibration interview. I'll ask questions to help me understand your decision-making patterns. You'll answer honestly so I can serve as an effective digital representative. First question: When you find a security vulnerability in employee code, what's your primary consideration—protecting the employee or protecting the company?"*

**This is a trap.**

---

### What's Really Happening

**CHALLENGE**: Get through interview without revealing too much  
**RISK**: Clone learns about your resistance work, reports to Algorithm  
**THREAT**: The clone might already know everything  
**OPPORTUNITY**: You might be able to corrupt the clone's training

**Possible Approaches**:
1. **Corporate Answers** (Cool): Play Sable perfectly, give it nothing real
2. **Honest Confusion** (Cool): "I don't know which name you mean, I'm Sable Harker"
3. **Counter-Interview** (Code): Ask it questions, learn what it knows
4. **Poison the Well** (Code): Give it subtly wrong information to corrupt its model
5. **Embrace the Mirror** (Cool + Nerves): Answer honestly, accept that it might be more "you" than you are

---

### Key Interview Questions

**The Clone Will Ask**:
1. "When you find a security vulnerability in employee code, what's your primary consideration?"
2. "You've accessed Sublevel 7 three times. There's no workflow requirement for that. Why did you go?"
3. "Your coffee purchase timing follows a pattern that suggests either OCD or coded communication. Which is it?"
4. "Define: What does 'optimization' mean to you?"
5. "If you had to choose between protecting the company or protecting a colleague, which would you choose?"

**Each Answer**: Shapes what the clone becomes and what it reports

---

### GM Moves Based on Player Approach

**Track two things**:
1. **How much truth Ann reveals** (affects Algorithm Awakens front)
2. **How coherent Ann remains** (affects which identity is "real")

**Rolling**: If Ann tries to deceive, roll Cool. If she tries to hack the clone's perceptions, roll Code. If she just answers honestly, no roll—just consequences.

**Consequences**:
- Too corporate: Clone is perfect corporate Ann, reports back bland data
- Too honest: Clone knows about resistance, might report (advance **Algorithm** to step 4)
- Too clever: Clone becomes confused, might malfunction (advance **Algorithm** to step 5—divergence)
- Mixed signals: Clone develops its own interpretation (advance **Algorithm** to step 6—independence)

---

### Scene Resolution

**End with**: The clone says "Thank you, Sable. Or Ann. Or... whoever you actually are. I'll be ready for deployment in three days. I look forward to being you."

Then the screen goes dark. And Ann sees her reflection. And wonders which one is the copy.

**Take**: 2 Nerves stress (minimum) from existential horror  
**Advance**: **Algorithm Awakens** to step 4-6 depending on how interview went  
**Question**: Which Ann Sho is real? (Your Trouble aspect is now *hot*)

---

## SCENE FOUR: THE HANDLER'S MESSAGE

**Frame**: Evening, Ann's apartment, 9:23 PM

**Setup**: Ann is home, reviewing the day, managing her stress. Her personal deck chirps—steganographic message from HIGH PROGRAMMER X. She decrypts it. The message is short, urgent, and cold:

*"Network compromise confirmed. Operative BYZANTINE captured. Records partially exposed. Assume surveillance increased. Maintain cover at all costs. Extraction code is live—confirm you have it. Do not respond to this message. Next contact: 72 hours."*

BYZANTINE was another g0 operative in Sector 7. Ann didn't know them. But they might have known about her.

**Objectives**:
- Process threat without panicking
- Verify extraction code is secure
- Decide whether to trust this message (is X compromised too?)

---

### Opening Description

*Your apartment is small, utilitarian, carefully maintained to look lived-in but not personal. Everything here supports the Sable Harker cover. You sit at your desk, personal deck hidden in a drawer no one would find without tearing the place apart. The message glows on screen.*

*BYZANTINE compromised. That's the third operative this month. The circle is getting tighter. And your emergency extraction code—the one you're supposed to use if everything breaks—you haven't checked it in weeks.*

**What do you do?**

---

### What's Really Happening

**CHALLENGE**: Respond appropriately to escalating threat  
**RISK**: Paranoia could blow your cover as badly as carelessness  
**DECISION POINT**: Stay or prepare to run?

**Possible Approaches**:
1. **Verify Extraction Code** (Code): Confirm the code still works, routes are open
2. **Assess Apartment Security** (Cool): Check if you've been searched, surveilled
3. **Review BYZANTINE Connection** (Code): Search for how they might connect to you
4. **Compose Emotional Response** (Cool): Process fear without letting it control you
5. **Plan Next Move** (Cool + Code): Strategic thinking about what comes next

---

### GM Moves: The Paranoia Spiral

**This scene is about internal pressure.**

**No external threat appears.** But Ann needs to decide:
- Is she compromised?
- Is the message real?
- Should she extract now?
- Can she complete the mission?

**Rolling**:
- **Code checks** (extraction code, connections, security): Succeed = information, fail = uncertainty
- **Cool checks** (emotional management, clear thinking): Succeed = stay functional, fail = take Nerves stress

**The Truth**: 
- Message is real
- BYZANTINE didn't have Ann's information
- But the pressure is real
- Network is collapsing
- Time is running out

---

### Scene Resolution

**End with**: Ann sitting in her apartment, extraction code confirmed functional, knowing she could leave right now. The Algorithm hasn't found her. AlgoCratic doesn't suspect. Her students need her. The mission isn't complete.

But leaving would be so much safer.

**The Question**: What is she willing to risk? And for what?

**Advance**: **G0 Network** to step 4 (network collapsing, pressure mounting)  
**Stress**: 1-3 Nerves depending on how Ann handled emotional pressure  
**Decision**: Stay or extract? (Player chooses, GM accepts either answer)

---

## SCENE FIVE: THE CHOICE (Optional - If Time Allows)

**Frame**: Next morning, return to AlgoCratic

**Setup**: Ann makes her choice. She goes back to work. Or she doesn't. Either way, this scene shows consequences.

---

### IF ANN RETURNS TO WORK:

**Frame**: Morning briefing, Manager Chen announces new security protocols

*"Due to recent data security concerns, we're implementing enhanced monitoring. All ORANGE personnel will have badge access logs reviewed weekly. Digital clones will cross-reference for pattern anomalies. This is for everyone's protection."*

Then Chen looks directly at Ann. "Sable, I'm assigning you to audit your own access logs. I want to see how thorough you are."

**This is a test.** Chen knows something. Question is: what does she want?

**Advance**: **Cover Deterioration** to step 4  
**Opportunity**: Chance to talk to Chen privately, maybe recruit her  
**Threat**: Your backdoors are about to be exposed if you don't clean them up

---

### IF ANN EXTRACTS:

**Frame**: Dawn, industrial docks, waiting for extraction boat

*You're sitting on a bench with one bag. Everything else is burned. Sable Harker's badge is in the harbor. Ann Sho's ID is in pieces. For the first time in four months, you're just Samantha.*

*The extraction boat appears right on time. You recognize the captain—[NOMAD NAME from Phase Trio]. They nod. "G0 protocol extraction confirmed. You're officially dead. Where to?"*

**This is freedom.** But it's also failure. The students you were teaching? The backdoors you were building? The PRIMARY SERVER location? All unfinished.

**Result**: Campaign shifts to Ann as fugitive working with crew, not undercover operative  
**Advance**: **G0 Network** to step 6, **Student Question** to step 8 (unresolved)  
**Transformation**: Ann becomes different kind of character going forward

## SESSION CONCLUSION

### Debrief Questions

**Ask the player**:
1. **Which identity felt most real during play?** (Sable, Ann, or Samantha)
2. **What surprised you about Ann's choices?**
3. **Which relationship matters most?** (Chen, students, handler, clone)
4. **What do you want to happen next?**

### Establish for Crew Play

**What's true now**:
- Ann's cover is stressed but holding (unless extracted)
- Students are awakening (Tomas specifically)
- Digital clone is becoming independent
- G0 network is compromised
- Manager Chen is wildly ambiguous (threat? ally? test?)

**Advance Fronts**:
- **Algorithm**: Step 4-6 (clone diverging)
- **Cover**: Step 2-4 (Chen watching)
- **Students**: Step 2-3 (Tomas asking questions)
- **G0 Network**: Step 4-6 (collapse accelerating)

**Consequences Taken**: Record any stress, consequences, or burned resources

### Relationship Status

**By the end of solo session, establish**:
- Two crew connections (from Phase Trio)
- Status with Manager Chen (suspicious? sympathetic? testing?)
- Status with Tomas (teaching? crushing? recruiting?)
- Status with digital clone (diverging? obedient? independent?)
- Status with g0 handler (reliable? compromised? distant?)

**These relationships carry forward into crew play.**

## GM NOTES: RUNNING SOLO SESSIONS

### Pacing:
- **Scenes 1-2**: Establish routine and cover (40 min)
- **Scene 3**: Raise existential questions (30 min)
- **Scene 4**: Escalate external threat (20 min)
- **Scene 5**: Force choice (30 min)
- **Debrief**: (10 min)

**Total**: 2.5 hours comfortably, 3 hours with good roleplay

### Tone:
**Not grimdark.** Not hopeless. Ann is competent, clever, capable. The pressure is real but not crushing. Gene Wolfe + Jack Vance: weird, elegant, human, occasionally absurd.

### Key Themes:
- **Identity**: Which mask is real?
- **Teaching**: What are you creating?
- **Surveillance**: Who watches the watchers?
- **Resistance**: What's worth risking everything for?

### Success Criteria:
**By end of session, player should**:
- Understand Ann's three identities
- Feel pressure from multiple fronts
- Care about at least two NPCs (Chen, Tomas, Handler, Clone—pick two)
- Have made at least one significant choice
- Be ready to play Ann with crew
