## HOW TO PLAY THE INFLUENCER

### YOU'RE GOOD AT:
- Technical work (you're actually skilled, not just pretty chrome)
- Social engineering through fame (everyone knows your face)
- Leveraging corporate sponsorships for access
- Looking good while working (it's not vanity, it's brand management)
- Turning disasters into content (everything is salvageable with right edit)

### YOU'RE BAD AT:
- Anonymity (3 million people know your face)
- Going off-grid (your chrome is monitored, your followers notice absence)
- Separating personal from professional (which one are you right now?)
- Resisting performance impulse (camera's not on, you're still performing)

### WHEN YOU:
- **Work for the audience**: Roll Cool. Success means great content AND job completion. Failure means you got one but not both.
- **Leverage your fame**: Roll Cool. Success means recognition helps. Failure means recognition is liability.
- **Use flagship chrome**: Roll Tech. Your gear is better than commercial grade. Enjoy the advantage. Remember the sponsorship obligations.
- **Go dark/offline**: Mark 1 Nerves stress per day. Your metrics drop. Sponsors get concerned. Followers speculate.

### YOUR PLAYBOOK MOVE

**Everything Is Content**: When you stream a job (requires audience permission), roll Cool. On 10+, choose two. On 7-9, choose one:
- Follower count spikes (+1 Resources from new sponsor offers)
- You look amazing doing it (+2 to next social interaction)
- Footage provides evidence you need later (perfect recall)
- Audience helps with real-time intel/support

On failure, you still get the content, but:
- Someone recognizes you who shouldn't
- Footage reveals more than you meant to show
- Sponsor demands you feature their product NOW
- Your producer says "we need to talk about that footage"
