## HOW TO PLAY THE NOMAD

### YOU'RE GOOD AT:
- Piloting (your boat is extension of you)
- Navigation (you know the routes, the currents, the dangers)
- Evasion (corporate patrols are predictable, you're not)
- Combat (when necessary, you handle it)
- Sensing trouble (the water tells you things before they happen)

### YOU'RE BAD AT:
- Staying in one place (the ocean calls, ports feel wrong)
- Trusting corporate authority (they've given you reasons not to)
- Saying no to people who need transport (it's a weakness)
- Admitting the boat needs repairs you can't afford (she'll make it, she always does)
- Anything requiring extensive code knowledge (you hire people for that)

### WHEN YOU:
- **Pilot your boat**: Roll Reflexes. On your vessel, you have +2 from neural link.
- **Navigate routes**: Roll Reflexes. Success means you find the path. Failure means complications.
- **Evade pursuit**: Roll Reflexes. You know patrol patterns. Use that knowledge.
- **Fight when cornered**: Roll Body or Reflexes. You were trained. You're dangerous when backed into corner.
- **Maintain the boat**: Roll Tech. Success means she keeps running. Failure means expensive problems.

### YOUR PLAYBOOK MOVE

**The Water Is Home**: When navigating pelagic routes in your boat, roll Reflexes. On 10+, choose three. On 7-9, choose one:
- You arrive faster than expected (save time)
- You avoid all patrols and checkpoints (save trouble)
- You find something valuable along the way (save money or gain intel)
- Your passengers are comfortable, impressed, or grateful (save face)

On failure, you arrive, but:
- Something broke during transit (boat takes damage)
- Patrols noticed you (trouble follows)
- Passengers are traumatized by rough journey (reputation hit)
