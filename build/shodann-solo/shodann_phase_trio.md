# PHASE TRIO: SHODANN'S HISTORY
## FATE-Style Phase Generation for Aspects and Relationships

*In FATE, characters establish their aspects through three phases: their own story, crossing paths with another character, and crossing paths with a third character. This creates interwoven histories and built-in relationships.*

---

## HOW PHASE TRIO WORKS

**Each phase answers questions and generates aspects:**

1. **Phase One (Your Story)**: Where you came from, what made you who you are → **HIGH CONCEPT** aspect
2. **Phase Two (First Crossing)**: How you met one crew member → **Connection** aspect
3. **Phase Three (Second Crossing)**: How you met another crew member → **Second Connection** aspect
4. **Phase Four (Trouble)**: What complication follows you → **TROUBLE** aspect

**Result**: Three aspects that define your character + two established relationships with crew

---

## PHASE ONE: SHODANN'S ORIGIN STORY

### THE QUESTIONS

**What broke your faith in the system?**
- NSA internship showed her surveillance state firsthand
- "Ethical disagreements" = they wanted her to build tools that would harm people
- Quit rather than compromise, chose poverty over complicity

**What taught you your skills?**
- DEF CON University (the real hackers' education)
- Bug bounty hunting (247K earned, 15 CVEs credited)
- Thesis: "Social Engineering Corporate AI" (she was studying The Algorithm before she knew it existed)

**What recruited you to resistance?**
- G0 approached her after she quit NSA
- They saw her thesis, her ethics, her capabilities
- Offered her a mission that mattered: teach the next generation to hack the planet

**How did you become three people?**
- G0 created "Sable Harker" identity (corporate background, clean history)
- She created "Ann Sho" as street identity (good underground ID, semi-public)
- "Samantha Hayes O'Day" became hidden true self

---

### PHASE ONE STORY

**Title**: *"The Education of Samantha O'Day"*

**Summary**: Fresh out of grad school, Samantha took an NSA internship thinking she'd protect people. Instead, she learned how surveillance works, who it targets, and why "security" means control. When they asked her to design predictive policing algorithms, she quit. Bug bounty work paid bills but felt hollow—she was patching holes in the same systems that oppressed people. Then G0 found her. They didn't want her to fix the system. They wanted her to teach people how to break it. She said yes.

**Aspect Generated**: **g0 Operative Playing Three Roles at Once**

*This HIGH CONCEPT captures: her resistance affiliation, her multiple identities, the constant performance of being someone she's not.*

## PHASE TWO: FIRST CROSSING

### THE SETUP

**You need to establish history with one crew member.** Choose from:
- The Nomad (boat captain, smuggler, ex-military)
- The Rigger (hardware specialist, tech savant)
- The Face (social operator, deal-maker)

**The Questions**:
- How did you meet them?
- What job went wrong (or surprisingly right)?
- Why do you trust them (or why don't you)?
- What do you owe each other?

---

### OPTION A: CROSSING WITH NOMAD

**Title**: *"Extraction Protocol Tango"*

**What Happened**:
Six months ago, Ann's first infiltration of AlgoCratic went sideways. She got the data but security locked down the facility. She had 20 minutes before they'd find her. G0 protocol: contact local extraction assets. She put out the call. A nomad named **[NOMAD NAME]** responded. They didn't ask questions. They didn't negotiate price. They just said "Dock 7, pier side, ten minutes." They got her out with five minutes to spare.

Ann paid triple rate—not required, but she knows what immediate response is worth. They're professional. Reliable. She's used them twice more since then. Each time, no questions, clean extraction. She doesn't know why they help resistance operators, but their boat always shows up.

**The Cost**: Security footage shows [NOMAD'S] boat. AlgoCratic has them flagged. If Ann uses them again, she's marking them hotter.

**Why This Matters**: 
- Establishes trust built on performance, not friendship
- Shows Ann values competence and reliability
- Creates tension: Using them helps her but endangers them
- Nomad knows Ann is resistance (saw what she was carrying)

**Aspect Generated**: **"[NOMAD] Gets Me Out When Things Go Wrong"**

*Invoke: When you need extraction, emergency transportation, or maritime know-how*  
*Compel: When using them increases heat on both of you, or when they're unavailable at critical moment*

---

### OPTION B: CROSSING WITH RIGGER

**Title**: *"The Hardware Vulnerability"*

**What Happened**:
Ann needed physical access to AlgoCratic's server room—specifically, needed to install a hardware keylogger that wouldn't be detected by The Algorithm's monitoring. Software approaches wouldn't work (too visible). She needed someone who could build something invisible. Underground contacts mentioned **[RIGGER NAME]**. They met in a machine shop at 3 AM.

Ann explained what she needed. Rigger said "That's not possible." Ann said "Make it possible." Rigger thought for thirty seconds, then: "Give me three days." They delivered in two. The device was brilliant—mimicked legitimate hardware at the component level, undetectable by conventional scanning.

Installation was smooth. The device worked for three weeks, captured everything, then self-destructed as designed. No trace. Professional. Elegant.

**The Cost**: Rigger knows Ann infiltrated a corp. Knows she's serious. Suspects bigger operation.

**Why This Matters**:
- Establishes respect for technical excellence
- Shows Ann can work with specialists outside her skillset
- Creates tension: Rigger might want in on bigger scores
- Ann has someone who can solve hardware problems she can't

**Aspect Generated**: **"[RIGGER] Builds Things The Algorithm Can't See"**

*Invoke: When you need custom hardware, technical solutions, or undetectable tools*  
*Compel: When Rigger's involvement complicates your cover, or when you need them but they're on another job*

---

### OPTION C: CROSSING WITH FACE

**Title**: *"The Credential Exchange"*

**What Happened**:
Ann's "Sable Harker" identity needed upgrading—more history, deeper background, references that would hold up under serious scrutiny. G0 could fake credentials, but not the subtle social proof that comes from being known. She needed someone who could manufacture reputation. Underground contacts pointed to **[FACE NAME]**.

Face didn't sell credentials. They sold reality. They said: "I'll introduce you to three people. By the end of the week, you'll have job references from two companies. Cost: one favor, to be determined later." Ann agreed.

Face delivered. Suddenly Sable Harker had former colleagues who remembered her, projects she'd worked on, professional relationships that existed in social memory. When AlgoCratic called references, real people confirmed Sable's history.

**The Cost**: Ann owes Face a favor. Terms undefined. That's how Face operates.

**Why This Matters**:
- Establishes Ann's cover required social engineering, not just documents
- Shows Face's power is creating convincing realities
- Creates tension: The favor will come due at an inconvenient time
- Face knows Ann's cover identity and how it was built

**Aspect Generated**: **"[FACE] Made Sable Harker Real—For a Price"**

*Invoke: When you need social proof, credentials, or someone to vouch for you*  
*Compel: When Face calls in their favor, or when they know too much about your cover*

## PHASE THREE: SECOND CROSSING

**Now establish history with a second crew member.** Use one of the options not chosen in Phase Two.

The format is identical:
1. **Choose which crew member** (Nomad, Rigger, or Face)
2. **Answer the questions** (How did you meet? What job happened? What do you owe each other?)
3. **Write a title and summary** (One or two paragraphs describing the crossing)
4. **Generate an aspect** (Connection with that character)

---

### EXAMPLE: SECOND CROSSING

If you chose **Nomad** in Phase Two, maybe Phase Three is:

**With Rigger**: *"The Bug in the Badge"*

Ann's security badge was standard AlgoCratic hardware—until Rigger modified it. Now it has double the access range, can spoof adjacent badges, and dumps encrypted logs to Ann's hidden deck every time she swipes in. Rigger installed it in exchange for data about AlgoCratic's security systems. Professional exchange. Both sides benefited.

**Aspect Generated**: **"My Badge Knows More Than I Do"**

## PHASE FOUR: TROUBLE

**Every character has a TROUBLE aspect.** This is the complication that follows you, the pressure that never stops, the problem you can't solve by being competent.

### THE QUESTIONS

**What threatens your mission?**
- Three identities are unstable (which one is real?)
- Cover could collapse any moment
- Students might expose her by accident
- G0 network compromised

**What threatens you personally?**
- Identity fragmentation ("Which Ann Sho is real?")
- Growing attachment to students (conscience vs mission)
- Isolation (can't trust anyone completely)
- Digital clone becoming more "her" than she is

**What can the GM use to complicate your life?**
- Make you choose between identities
- Put students in danger
- Force you to maintain cover when it conflicts with mission
- Have your clone do something you wouldn't do

---

### SHODANN'S TROUBLE

**Aspect**: **"Which Ann Sho Is Real?"**

**What This Means**:
- You've been performing so long you're not sure which identity is authentic
- Sable Harker is the comfortable lie (career, safety, belonging)
- Ann Sho is the useful lie (reputation, freedom, connection)
- Samantha O'Day might be the biggest lie (noble cause, righteous mission)
- What if resistance is just another performance? What if corporate life is actually what you want?

**Invoke When**:
- You use identity confusion strategically
- You switch personas to gain advantage
- You're questioned and misdirect by being "honestly confused"
- You need to be multiple people simultaneously

**Compel When**:
- You forget your cover story
- Someone from one identity meets someone from another
- You have to choose between identities in crisis
- Your clone is more coherent than you are
- You question your own authenticity in critical moment

## GM NOTES: USING PHASE TRIO RELATIONSHIPS

### In Play:
**Invoke these connections regularly**:
- "I call [Nomad] for extraction—we've done this before"
- "I ask [Rigger] if they can modify my neural deck"
- "I owe [Face] a favor—maybe this is when they collect"

**Compel these connections for drama**:
- "[Nomad's] boat is burned because of you"
- "[Rigger] is captured by AlgoCratic and might give you up"
- "[Face] needs you to attend a high-society function that risks your cover"

### Between Sessions:
**Develop relationships**: Each crew member becomes more complex as play progresses  
**Add new NPCs to the web**: Crew members have their own contacts who intersect with Ann  
**Create triangles**: Ann + Crew Member + NPC = drama
