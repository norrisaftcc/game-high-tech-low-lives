## THE LAYERS

**What The Audience Sees**: KaidaV, tech influencer. 3.2M followers across platforms. Sponsored by Apex Chrome, StreamLine Networks, and six other corps. She runs data, cracks systems, looks good doing it. Every job is content. Every success is a brand moment. Aspirational lifestyle meets technical competence.

**What The Corps See**: Kaida Voss, contractor. Security consultant. Penetration tester. She finds vulnerabilities, demonstrates exploits, gets paid. The streaming is marketing—it brings clients. The chrome is investment. The followers are proof of reach. Useful asset. Manageable liability.

**What You Know**: You're good. Actually good. The chrome isn't just aesthetic—it works. The followers aren't fake—they're real people watching you do real work. But somewhere between performing competence and being competent, the line blurred. You're not sure which version of you is authentic anymore. Maybe both. Maybe neither.

*The audience expects a show. The corps expect results. You deliver both. The cost is knowing which one you're performing at any given moment.*

## ASPECTS

**High Concept**: **Apex-Sponsored Runner With Real Credentials**

You're on payroll. Multiple payrolls. Apex Chrome sponsors your augments. StreamLine Networks hosts your content. DataCorp pays for vulnerability assessments. Each corp thinks they own a piece of you. None of them realize you're playing them against each other for access.

*Invoke*: When using corporate sponsorship as cover, when leveraging follower count as social proof, when your chrome is literally advertisement-grade cutting edge.

*Compel*: When sponsors demand you showcase their product mid-run, when rival corps notice you're working for competitors, when your follower count makes you recognizable in situations requiring anonymity.

**Trouble**: **The Performance Is The Person Is The Performance**

Every job is streamed. Every interaction is content. You've been performing yourself for so long you're not sure where Kaida Voss ends and KaidaV begins. The chrome that makes you effective also makes you marketable. The skills that got you sponsors also trapped you in their expectations.

*Compel*: When staying in character means compromising the mission, when you have to choose between good content and good tactics, when someone who knows you from streams recognizes you, when you catch yourself performing even when cameras are off.

**Connection**: **Lian Chen, My Producer, Knows Too Much**

Lian edits your streams. Cuts the footage. Manages the sponsors. Knows which moments you cut from public feeds. Knows which jobs you don't stream. Knows the difference between performance and reality because they see both. They have leverage. They're also the only person you trust with that leverage.

*Invoke*: When you need footage edited before release, when sponsors need handling, when someone needs to cover for you, when you need someone who knows the real you.

*Compel*: When Lian's loyalty is tested by better offers, when they're targeted to get to you, when they question whether you're compromising too much for the brand.
