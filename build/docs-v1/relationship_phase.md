# PHASE FOUR: RELATIONSHIPS

After creating your character's aspects, stats, and chrome, establish how you know the other crew members. No heist crew works together for the first time—you have history. That history creates trust, tension, or both.

## HOW IT WORKS

Each player pairs with another player. Together, choose one of the two prompts provided OR create your own inspired by them. Write 2-3 sentences describing the shared history. This becomes part of both characters' backgrounds.

**Format**: "[Character A] and [Character B]: [Brief description of shared history]"

**Important**: These relationships don't have to be positive. Complicated history makes better gameplay. Professional respect mixed with personal tension is ideal for heists.

---

## ANN SHO & KAIDA VOSS

Choose one or create your own:

### Option A: The Audit That Wasn't
**Prompt**: Ann audited Kaida's security setup six months ago while working for AlgoCratic. Found three serious vulnerabilities. Instead of reporting them, she sent Kaida encrypted messages detailing the flaws. Kaida fixed them and hasn't forgotten—but also wonders what Ann wants in return.

**Tension**: Ann has leverage (could have destroyed Kaida's credibility). Kaida suspects ulterior motives. Trust exists but it's professional, not personal.

### Option B: The Stream That Got Edited
**Prompt**: Kaida accidentally captured Ann's face during a corporate infiltration stream. The footage showed Ann somewhere she wasn't supposed to be. Producer Lian flagged it before it went public. Ann knows Kaida's producer has that footage. They've never discussed it directly.

**Tension**: Kaida has evidence of Ann's activities (via Lian). Ann knows Kaida saw her. Neither acknowledges it. Both know the other knows.

## ANN SHO & REYES TOVAN

Choose one or create your own:

### Option A: The Data Run
**Prompt**: Ann needed extraction from AlgoCratic facility at 3 AM. No questions asked, immediate departure. Reyes got the call through his nomad network. He ran her out on the Mirasol before corporate security caught up. She paid triple rate plus repair costs. He still doesn't know what she was running from.

**Tension**: Reyes knows Ann burns hot when compromised. Ann knows Reyes is reliable but also wanted by the same corps she's infiltrating. Their mutual illegality creates uneasy alliance.

### Option B: The Navigation Code Trade
**Prompt**: Ann had access to updated Fujikara-Pacific patrol routes—classified data from AlgoCratic's corporate clients. Reyes needed those routes to avoid his former employers. They traded: navigation data for forged corporate credentials. Neither asked questions about why the other needed what they needed.

**Tension**: Both know the other has criminal needs. Both provided illegal assistance. Mutually assured exposure creates trust through shared liability.

## KAIDA VOSS & REYES TOVAN

Choose one or create your own:

### Option A: The Rescue Stream
**Prompt**: Kaida's stream equipment failed during underwater infiltration. She was trapped in flooded sector with 20 minutes of air. Reyes happened to be running cargo nearby, heard the distress call. Pulled her out, didn't charge rescue fee. She streamed the rescue anyway—3.2 million people watched Reyes save her life. He's recognizable now. He's not happy about it.

**Tension**: Kaida owes him her life. Reyes blames her for making his face famous, complicating his illegal smuggling. She thinks she made him a hero. He thinks she made him a target.

### Option B: The Sponsor Deal Rejection
**Prompt**: Apex Chrome approached Reyes about sponsoring the Mirasol—free upgrades, maintenance coverage, featured in Kaida's streams as "the getaway boat." Reyes refused immediately. Kaida pushed the deal anyway, thinking she was helping. He shut her down hard. She still doesn't understand why freedom matters more than free chrome.

**Tension**: Class/philosophy divide. Kaida sees sponsorships as smart business. Reyes sees them as chains. She thinks he's stubborn. He thinks she's naive about what corporate backing costs. They work together professionally but don't understand each other's choices.

## REYES TOVAN & MARCUS KAIN

**Pre-established** (No choice needed—this relationship is canon):

**The Fixer Who Vouched**: Marcus gave Reyes his first smuggling job after the desertion. Vouched for him to clients when nobody trusted an ex-military runner with stolen boat. Didn't ask questions about the theft or the 47 refugees. Just said "you've got the skills, you need the work, I need reliable transport." Five years later, they're still working together. Marcus knows Reyes won't betray clients. Reyes knows Marcus plays all sides but hasn't burned him yet.

**Relationship Dynamic**: Professional trust built on repeated success. Marcus calls when he needs reliable extraction. Reyes answers because Marcus pays fair and provides good intel. Neither pretends it's friendship. Both prefer it that way.

## CREATING YOUR OWN RELATIONSHIP

If the prompts don't fit, create your own using this template:

**The [Event/Situation]**: [One sentence: what happened]

[2-3 sentences: How it went down, what each character did, what the outcome was]

**Tension/Trust**: [One sentence: What complicated or strengthened the relationship]

**Good relationships include**:
- Specific event (not vague history)
- Both characters made choices
- Something unresolved or unspoken
- Reason to work together again despite (or because of) complications

**Examples**:
- "She saved my life but I never paid her back"
- "We pulled a job that went sideways—we got out, our partner didn't"
- "I have information they need, they have access I need"
- "We both know something about each other that could be leveraged"

## ROLEPLAYING RELATIONSHIPS

**During Play**:
- Reference the shared history naturally ("Remember Yosemite Station?")
- Let complications from past inform present choices
- Tension creates better scenes than perfect harmony
- Professional crews can disagree about methods while respecting competence

**Invoking Relationships**:
You can invoke relationship history like any aspect:
- Spend Fate Point to reference shared experience for +2
- GM can compel relationship history to create complications
- "Remember how that last job ended? This looks similar..."

**Evolution**:
Relationships change through play. The person who saved your life might betray you next session. The rival you resented might become trusted partner. Let the fiction evolve based on what happens at the table.

## GM NOTES: USING RELATIONSHIPS

**In Planning Scenes**: Characters reference how they worked together before
- "Last time we didn't account for [X], let's not make that mistake again"

**In Complications**: Past history creates present problems
- "Kaida's face gets recognized—Reyes blames the rescue stream"

**In Trust Moments**: Shared experiences inform risky decisions  
- "Ann asks Reyes for extraction again—same terms, same trust"

**In Betrayals**: Relationships make betrayal meaningful
- If someone turns, the history makes it hurt

Relationships aren't flavor text. They're loaded guns on the table. Use them.
