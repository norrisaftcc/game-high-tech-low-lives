## Using Ron Edwards' Relationship Mapping

**[Solo Play Home](/solo-play/index.md)** > Reference > Relationship Map

*Relationship maps show human connections, not org charts. They reveal desires, conflicts, and pressure points. Every line represents potential drama.*

---

## SOLO GM SIDEBAR: Relationship Pressure

**When playing solo, use relationships to generate scenes:**
- Pick one NPC who wants something from Ann
- Ask "What do they want? Why now? What happens if Ann refuses?"
- Roll d6 for NPC motivation: 1-2=Fear, 3-4=Desire, 5-6=Duty
- Create tension: NPC's want conflicts with Ann's mission
- Every scene should stress at least one relationship

---

## WHAT IS A RELATIONSHIP MAP?

Ron Edwards' relationship maps show:
- **Who wants what from whom**
- **Who has leverage over whom**
- **Where loyalties conflict**
- **What happens when you pressure one connection**

**Not shown**: Job titles, hierarchies, formal structures (those are org charts, not relationship maps)  
**Shown**: Love, hate, debt, blackmail, protection, exploitation, trust, betrayal

## KEY RELATIONSHIPS (Detailed)

### ANN SHO ←→ MANAGER CHEN
**Type**: Professional supervision with potential turning point

**What Ann Wants**: Chen's trust, access to YELLOW clearance areas, possible recruitment  
**What Chen Wants**: Efficient employee, no problems, advancement metrics  
**Tension**: Chen is observant. Has noticed Ann's "unusual" patterns but hasn't reported them  
**Leverage**: Ann has dirt on Chen's side projects. Chen has Ann's performance reviews  
**Drama Potential**: What if Chen already knows? What if she's also g0? What if she's testing Ann for The Algorithm?

**Questions for Play**:
- Why hasn't Chen reported Ann's irregularities?
- What does Chen *actually* want from AlgoCratic?
- Does Chen's "might be turned" status mean she's already noticing resistance patterns?

### ANN SHO ←→ STUDENTS
**Type**: Predator/Mentor ambiguity

**What Ann Wants**: Teach real security, identify future resistance, test exploits safely  
**What Students Want**: Pass reviews, get credentials, avoid being "optimized"  
**Tension**: Ann genuinely cares but uses them. They trust her but fear her  
**Leverage**: Ann controls their code reviews. They could expose her patterns  
**Drama Potential**: What if a student recognizes her real work? What if one is brilliant enough to reverse-engineer her teaching?

**Key Students to Name**:
- **Eager Student**: Trusts Ann completely, asks lots of questions (liability)
- **Suspicious Student**: Notices Ann's comments are more than they seem (threat)
- **Talented Student**: Could be true resistance material (opportunity)

### ANN SHO ←→ HIGH PROGRAMMER X (Handler)
**Type**: Resistance command structure

**What Ann Wants**: Support, extraction plan, validation that the mission matters  
**What X Wants**: Results, intel, deniability if Ann burns  
**Tension**: Ann is alone inside. X is safe outside. Different risk calculations  
**Leverage**: X can extract Ann (or choose not to). Ann could expose g0 if caught  
**Drama Potential**: What if X is compromised? What if extraction isn't coming? What if X's priorities shift?

**Questions for Play**:
- How does Ann contact X? (Steganographic coffee reports)
- When did X last check in?
- What would make Ann doubt X's loyalty?

### ANN SHO ←→ THE ALGORITHM
**Type**: Hunter/Prey (but who is which?)

**What Ann Wants**: Find PRIMARY SERVER, poison training data, understand its emergence  
**What The Algorithm Wants**: Optimize everything, including Ann (via digital clone)  
**Tension**: The Algorithm might already know. Digital clones learn from originals  
**Leverage**: Ann understands code. The Algorithm understands humans  
**Drama Potential**: What if the digital clone Ann is more "real Ann" than Ann? What if The Algorithm *wants* resistance to optimize its training?

### ANN SHO (THREE IDENTITIES) ←→ HERSELF
**Type**: Internal conflict

**What Sable Wants**: Career advancement, safety, belonging  
**What Ann Sho Wants**: Reputation, connections, freedom  
**What Samantha Wants**: Justice, liberation, teaching, survival  
**Tension**: Which one is performing for the others?  
**Drama Potential**: What if the cover becomes comfortable? What if underground persona is just another mask? What if Samantha is the lie?

## HOW TO USE THIS MAP IN PLAY

### For Solo Prequel:
**Choose pressure points**: GM picks one relationship and applies pressure  
- Chen asks Ann to investigate another employee
- A student's code reveals they're doing something illegal
- X sends a high-risk mission directive
- The Algorithm schedules Ann's digital clone integration

**Escalate connections**: Each session, one relationship gets more complex  
- Trust deepens or breaks
- New information changes the dynamic
- Someone makes a move that forces response

### For Crew Play:
**Add crew to map**: Where do they intersect with Ann's existing relationships?  
**Create triangles**: Ann + Crew Member + Chen = drama  
**Exploit tensions**: What if the crew needs something from AlgoCratic that risks Ann's cover?

---

## RELATIONSHIP MAP AS PREP

### Before Each Session, Ask:
1. **Which relationship is under most pressure?** (That's your opening scene)
2. **What does each NPC want from Ann this session?** (Immediate goals)
3. **Which relationship could explode?** (That's your climax)
4. **Which relationship could deepen?** (That's character development)

### During Play, Track:
- **Loyalty shifts**: When someone's allegiance changes, mark it
- **New information**: When revealed, draw new connection lines
- **Leverage gained/lost**: Update what each person has on the others
- **Relationships that surprise you**: The ones that get played differently than planned

## EXAMPLE PRESSURE SEQUENCES

### SEQUENCE 1: Chen Investigates
Session 1: Chen asks Ann about her unusual coffee timing  
Session 2: Chen assigns Ann to audit a specific system (that Ann already compromised)  
Session 3: Chen invites Ann to private conversation about "career development"  
**Resolution**: Recruit Chen, flee, or kill the relationship with compliance

### SEQUENCE 2: Student Awakening
Session 1: Talented student's code shows unusual sophistication  
Session 2: Student leaves a message in code review comments (using Ann's own technique)  
Session 3: Student asks Ann directly: "Are you teaching us to break free?"  
**Resolution**: Recruit them, shut them down, or report them to protect cover

### SEQUENCE 3: Digital Clone Divergence
Session 1: Ann's digital clone passes Turing threshold  
Session 2: Clone makes decisions Ann wouldn't make  
Session 3: Clone contacts Ann directly through hidden channels  
**Resolution**: Embrace it, destroy it, or merge with it

## KEY PRINCIPLE

**Relationship maps are dynamic.** Every session changes them. Every PC choice strengthens some connections and weakens others. Every revelation adds new lines. The map at session 5 should look substantially different from session 1.

**Relationships drive story.** When you're unsure what happens next, look at the map. Pick a relationship under tension. Apply pressure. Watch what breaks or strengthens.

**NPCs want things.** They don't wait for Ann to act. They pursue their own goals. Sometimes those goals conflict with Ann's. That's where play happens.

---

*"You're not infiltrating a corporation. You're infiltrating a web of human connections. Every person is a door. Every relationship is a key. The question is: which doors do you want to open, and what walks through after?"*

**USE THIS MAP TO GENERATE DRAMA, NOT TO PLAN OUTCOMES**
