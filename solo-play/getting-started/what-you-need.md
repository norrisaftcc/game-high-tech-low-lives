## FATE DICE BASICS

**4dF = 4 Fate Dice**

Each die shows: **[ + ]** (plus), **[ - ]** (minus), or **[ blank ]** (zero)

Roll 4dF, count the symbols:
- 2 plusses, 1 minus, 1 blank = +1
- 3 plusses, 1 blank = +3
- 2 minuses, 2 blanks = -2

**Total Range**: -4 to +4 (bell curve around 0)

**Add your stat**: Code +3 + roll of +1 = total of +4

**Compare to difficulty**:
- 0-2 = Easy
- 3-4 = Moderate
- 5-6 = Hard
- 7+ = Very Hard

**Success**: Total equals or exceeds difficulty
**Success with Style**: Total exceeds by 3+

### Don't Have Fate Dice?
Use standard d6 dice:
- 1-2 = minus
- 3-4 = blank
- 5-6 = plus

Or use online roller: https://perchance.org/fate-dice

## STRESS SYSTEM

**Shodann has three stress tracks**:

**MEAT** (Physical): 3 boxes [□ □ □]
**NERVES** (Mental): 4 boxes [□ □ □ □] (Cool +1 bonus)
**SYSTEMS** (Digital): 3 boxes [□ □ □]

**When you take stress**:
1. Check one box (or multiple for big hits)
2. If track is full, take a **Consequence**
3. Mild consequence = -2 to related rolls
4. Moderate consequence = -4 to related rolls
5. Severe consequence = -6 to related rolls

**Consequences heal slowly** (Mild = end of scene, Moderate = end of session, Severe = multiple sessions)

**Example**: Chen confronts you about badge access. GM says "Take 2 Nerves stress."
- You mark 2 boxes on NERVES track [■ ■ □ □]
- If NERVES was already full, you'd take consequence: "Paranoid About Surveillance" (Mild)

## ASPECTS QUICK START

**Aspects are true things about your character.**

**Shodann's Core Aspects**:
- **High Concept**: "g0 Operative Playing Three Roles at Once"
- **Trouble**: "Which Ann Sho Is Real?"
- **Connection**: (Created during Phase Trio)
- **Free Aspect**: (Your choice)

### Using Aspects

**Invoke** (spend 1 Fate Point):
- +2 to roll, OR
- Reroll dice
- *"My High Concept helps here because I'm used to lying"* (+2 to Cool roll)

**Compel** (GM or you suggest, get 1 Fate Point):
- Aspect creates complication
- *"Your Trouble compels you to forget which cover story you're using"* (Accept = gain Fate point + complication)

**Start each session with 3 Fate Points**

## THE FOUR FRONTS

**Fronts are threats with countdown clocks.** They advance unless you stop them.

**Shodann's Fronts**:

1. **The Algorithm Awakens** (12 steps)
   - Digital clone becoming independent
   - Emergent AI gaining consciousness

2. **Cover Deterioration** (10 steps)
   - Manager Chen noticing patterns
   - Three identities collapsing

3. **The Student Question** (8 steps)
   - Students awakening to resistance
   - Teaching or predation?

4. **G0 Network Exposure** (12 steps)
   - Resistance network compromised
   - Pressure closing in

**Each front has countdown clocks.** Check boxes as scenes advance threats.

**You can't stop all fronts.** Choose which ones matter most.

## SESSION STRUCTURE

**Solo sessions use structured scenes**:

**Scene One**: Coffee Dead Drop (establish routine + pressure)
**Scene Two**: Student Question (teaching moment + moral choice)
**Scene Three**: Digital Clone Interview (existential horror)
**Scene Four**: Handler's Message (external threat escalation)
**Scene Five**: The Choice (decision point)

**Each scene**:
- Has clear objective
- Presents choices
- Advances 1-2 fronts
- Takes 20-40 minutes

**Total session**: 2-3 hours

---

## DECISION FRAMEWORK

**Every scene offers choices. Use this framework:**

1. **Read the setup** - Understand situation
2. **Consider options** - Usually 3-5 approaches
3. **Choose based on character** - What would Ann do?
4. **Roll if needed** - GM tells you when
5. **Accept consequences** - Mark stress, advance fronts
6. **Move to next scene** - Story continues

**There are no wrong choices.** Only interesting consequences.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**"I don't understand FATE"**
→ Read only: Dice basics, Stress system, Aspects (above). That's enough to start.

**"I don't have Fate Dice"**
→ Use d6 conversion (1-2=minus, 3-4=blank, 5-6=plus) or online roller

**"This seems complicated"**
→ Use Pre-Generated Shodann + 5-Minute Quickstart. Skip theory, learn by playing.

**"How do I make choices without a GM?"**
→ Session Guide provides decision points. Fronts create pressure. You decide what matters to Ann.

**"What if I make the wrong choice?"**
→ No wrong choices. Some advance certain fronts. That's the point - pressure creates story.

## SOLO GM TECHNIQUES (Brief)

**You'll use these during play** (Session Guide explains when):

**Fronts** - Threats advance on timers. Check boxes when scene ends.
**Oracles** - Roll dice to answer yes/no questions (1-2=No, 3-4=Maybe, 5-6=Yes)
**NPC Motivations** - Each NPC wants something. Pick from their list.
**Pressure Points** - When stuck, pick a relationship and apply pressure.

**Don't worry about mastering these.** The Session Guide walks you through.
