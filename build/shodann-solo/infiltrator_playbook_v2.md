## THE INFILTRATOR

*You've been inside. Corporate security, facility audits, penetration testing—at some point you learned that the best way to break in is to be invited. You have credentials. You know the systems. You left yourself ways back in. The question is: which identity is really you?*

## CHROME (Choose 2)

Each piece of chrome shifts your stress tracks. You enhance access, not combat.

### ☐ Neural Deck Port
*Direct brain-to-system interface. No external hardware needed.*
- **Effect**: +1 to Code when directly connected to systems
- **Stress Shift**: +2 Systems, -1 Meat
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: You can't jack in. Roll Code at -2 until repaired.

### ☐ Pattern Recognition Suite
*Enhanced visual processing. You see connections others miss.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Code when analyzing systems for vulnerabilities
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (information overload)
- **Maintenance**: 300 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Migraine-level sensory input. Take 1 Nerves stress per scene until fixed.

### ☐ Corporate Credentials (Chrome Version)
*Subdermal RFID that spoofs legitimate corporate access.*
- **Effect**: Pass as corporate employee in many facilities. Rotate identities.
- **Risk**: If scanned deeply, shows as fake. Also shows as chrome to detectors.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems
- **Maintenance**: 200 credits/month (updating credentials)
- **When it breaks**: Locked to one identity. That identity might be burned.

### ☐ Subvocal Comm
*Silent communication via throat vibrations. Looks like thinking.*
- **Effect**: Communicate without being overheard. Encrypted channel.
- **Stress Shift**: None (minimal installation)
- **Maintenance**: 100 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: You revert to speaking out loud. Compel this when bad timing.

### ☐ Memory Buffer
*External storage for experiences. Perfect recall when needed.*
- **Effect**: Once per session, remember any scene perfectly. Review it frame by frame.
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (disconnection from present)
- **Maintenance**: 250 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Your memories feel unreliable. -1 to Cool until repaired.

### ☐ Social Analysis Suite
*Read micro-expressions, vocal stress, pupil dilation.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when reading people's emotions or detecting lies
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (everyone is transparent, you can't unsee it)
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Reading people wrong. Paranoia. -2 to social rolls.

**After choosing chrome, calculate your final stress tracks.**

## TALENTS (Choose 2)

### ☐ Backdoor Specialist (4 points)
Once per session, automatically succeed at infiltrating a system you've previously audited or accessed. No roll needed. You left yourself a way in. Describe what you did when you were there before.

### ☐ Corporate Camouflage (2 points)
+2 to Cool when maintaining cover in corporate environments. You know how to look busy while doing nothing, how to belong, how to be forgettable.

### ☐ Quick Exit Protocol (2 points)
When caught somewhere you shouldn't be, roll Cool. On success, you have a plausible excuse ready. On failure, you still get out but someone remembers you.

### ☐ Information Broker (2 points)
You've got contacts. Once per session, declare you know someone who has the information you need. GM says who and what it costs. You can introduce helpful NPCs into the fiction.

### ☐ Emergency Purge (2 points)
When Systems stress fills, clear all Systems stress by taking Moderate Consequence: *Chrome Shutdown*. One augment goes dark until repaired.

### ☐ Familiar Ground (2 points)
When entering a location type you've infiltrated before (corporate office, research lab, security facility), automatically know the standard layout, security protocols, and likely vulnerabilities. You've been here—or somewhere exactly like it.

## HOW TO PLAY THE INFILTRATOR

### YOU'RE GOOD AT:
- Getting into places you shouldn't be
- Finding system vulnerabilities
- Maintaining cover stories and false identities
- Analyzing security (physical and digital)
- Leaving backdoors for future access
- Blending into corporate environments
- Knowing who to talk to for information

### YOU'RE BAD AT:
- Direct confrontation (you sneak, not fight)
- Trusting anyone fully
- Maintaining a single identity
- Walking away from a security challenge
- Explaining what you do to non-technical people
- Staying in one place too long
- Real relationships (everyone might be compromised)

### WHEN YOU ROLL:
- **Body**: You probably avoid this (hire muscle)
- **Reflexes**: Quick exits, dodging security, physical infiltration
- **Code**: Hacking, bypassing security, analyzing systems, finding exploits
- **Tech**: Understanding hardware, maintaining your deck, jury-rigging access
- **Cool**: Maintaining cover, lying convincingly, staying calm when questioned

## THE INFILTRATOR MOVE: FAMILIAR GROUND

Once per session, when entering a facility or system, you can declare:

**"I've done this before—exactly this."**

You have. Whether you audited this facility, tested their security, or worked here under another name, you know:
- The standard security protocols
- Common vulnerabilities
- Where the cameras are (or should be)
- Which employees have access to what
- How to blend in

You automatically succeed at initial infiltration. The GM tells you one thing that's different from what you expected—something changed, something's wrong, something's new. That's your complication.

You're in. Now what do you do with that access?

## QUESTIONS TO ANSWER (Discover through play)

You don't need answers now. Find them during the game:

1. **What corporation did you work for?** What did you do there?
2. **What did you find that made you leave?** Do they know you have it?
3. **How many identities do you maintain?** Which one is closest to real?
4. **What system did you crack that you're most proud of?** Most ashamed of?
5. **Who's hunting you?** Do they want you back or dead?
6. **What backdoor have you left that could destroy someone?** When will you use it?
7. **If you could only keep one identity, which would it be?** Why?

## EXAMPLE: ANN SHO

**High Concept**: *Ex-Corporate Security Auditor with Backdoors Everywhere*  
**Trouble**: *AlgoCratic-Fujikara Wants Their Proprietary Access Codes Back*  
**Connection**: *My Sister Still Works Sidereal Security—She Doesn't Know What I Do*

**Stats**: Body +1, Reflexes +2, Code +3, Tech +2, Cool +1

**Chrome**:
- Neural Deck Port (+2 Systems, -1 Meat)
- Corporate Credentials (+1 Systems)

**Final Stress**: Meat ▢▢ | Nerves ▢▢▢ | Systems ▢▢▢▢▢▢

**Talents**:
- Backdoor Specialist (4 points)
- Information Broker (2 points)

**Gear**: Three sets of corporate credentials (AlgoCratic, Meridian Holdings, Sidereal Industries), portable deck, professional wardrobe, encrypted storage, 2,000 credits

**Notes**: Ann worked security audits for AlgoCratic-Fujikara, one of the major pelagic corps operating between Golgotha and Sidereal. She tested their security by breaking it. She was exceptional at it. Too good. She found things she shouldn't have—experimental projects, black budget operations, things AlgoCratic does that violate every treaty. She left. She took the access codes with her. Now she audits for freelance clients, but AlgoCratic knows she has their secrets. Her sister works Sidereal Station security and doesn't know Ann's on corporate wanted lists. Every time Ann uses her backdoors, she risks leaving a trace. She does it anyway. Some things need to be exposed.

## NARRATIVE AUTHORITY: WHAT YOU CAN DECLARE

As the Infiltrator, you have explicit permission to introduce these elements into the shared fiction:

**Access**: "I have credentials that let me try to pass here"  
**Prior Knowledge**: "I've been here before" or "I audited this facility"  
**Backdoors**: "When I was here last time, I left myself a way in"  
**Contacts**: "I know someone in this organization who owes me"  
**Vulnerabilities**: "I can see how this security fails"

You don't ask "Can I get in?"—you declare how you're already positioned to do so, then deal with complications.
