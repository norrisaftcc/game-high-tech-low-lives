*You are professional violence. Corporate muscle, gang soldier, private security—somewhere along the line, you learned that problems get solved with presence, intimidation, and controlled brutality. ## CHROME (Choose 2)

You chrome for combat and survival, not finesse.

### ☐ Subdermal Armor Plating
*Woven carbon lattice under your skin. Bullets hurt less.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Body when resisting physical damage
- **Stress Shift**: +2 Meat, -1 Nerves (you stop feeling things the same way)
- **Maintenance**: 500 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Plating shifts, causes internal bleeding. Take 1 Meat stress per scene until repaired.

### ☐ Reflex Augmentation
*Neural accelerators and muscle memory overrides. You move before thinking.*
- **Effect**: +1 to Reflexes in combat situations
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (reaction precedes thought)
- **Maintenance**: 450 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Delayed responses, muscle spasms. Take -2 to Reflexes until fixed.

### ☐ Adrenaline Regulator
*Synthetic adrenal glands. Fear is optional.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Cool when facing fear or intimidation
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Nerves (you stop recognizing danger appropriately)
- **Maintenance**: 400 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Either nothing (dangerously calm) or panic flood (terrified). Compel this.

### ☐ Tactical Combat HUD
*Threat assessment, targeting overlay, battlefield awareness.*
- **Effect**: +1 to Reflexes when you can see opponents clearly
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Meat (eye strain, neural load)
- **Maintenance**: 300 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Visual glitches, false threat markers, migraine-level pain. -2 to sight-based rolls.

### ☐ Enhanced Musculature
*Myomer fiber weave. You hit harder.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Body for raw strength, breaking things
- **Stress Shift**: +2 Systems, -2 Meat (strain on organic frame)
- **Maintenance**: 600 credits/month (high bio-integration cost)
- **When it breaks**: Muscle tears, chrome rejection. Can't use enhanced strength; pain on exertion.

### ☐ Dermal Toxin Filters
*Blood scrubbers. Poisons don't work well.*
- **Effect**: +2 to Body against toxins, drugs, chemical weapons
- **Stress Shift**: +1 Systems, -1 Meat (filter load)
- **Maintenance**: 350 credits/month
- **When it breaks**: Toxin backlog floods system. First exposure after break is *worse*.

## TALENTS (Choose 2)

### ☐ Combat Veteran (4 points)
In extended contests involving violence, you can absorb one additional exchange before consequences. Once per session, automatically succeed at reading a combat situation—you know who's dangerous, who's scared, who's going to break. No roll required.

### ☐ Presence (2 points)
You can achieve through intimidation what others achieve through violence. +2 to Cool when threatening harm without delivering it. People believe you'll do it. They're right.

### ☐ Take the Hit (2 points)
When an ally would take physical stress, you can interpose yourself and take it instead. Roll Body—on success, you absorb it cleanly. On failure, you both take stress.

### ☐ Professional (2 points)
You've done this a thousand times. +2 to Cool when following orders, executing plans, or maintaining professional composure during violence. You don't let emotions interfere with the job.

### ☐ Close Quarters Specialist (2 points)
In confined spaces (corridors, rooms, tight quarters), you're at your best. +2 to Reflexes when fighting in close combat situations. You know how to use terrain.

### ☐ Read the Room (2 points)
Years of security work taught you to spot trouble. +2 to Cool when assessing threat levels in social situations, identifying who's armed, who's nervous, who's in charge.

## HOW TO PLAY THE ENFORCER

### YOU'RE GOOD AT:
- Direct confrontation
- Protecting people through violence or threat
- Assessing combat situations instantly
- Maintaining professional composure during chaos
- Intimidating people into compliance
- Taking physical punishment
- Following plans under fire

### YOU'RE BAD AT:
- Subtlety (you're built to be noticed)
- De-escalation (every tool looks like a hammer when you're a hammer)
- Technical work (you break things, not fix them)
- Social finesse (people are either clients, threats, or civilians)
- Walking away from a fight
- Trusting people who aren't crew
- Separating personal from professional

### WHEN YOU ROLL:
- **Body**: Absorbing damage, raw strength, physical endurance
- **Reflexes**: Combat response, dodging, quick draws, initiative
- **Code**: You probably don't (hire a hacker)
- **Tech**: Basic weapon maintenance, gear checks, tactical assessment
- **Cool**: Intimidation, threat assessment, professional composure, reading people for violence potential

## THE ENFORCER MOVE: HOLD THE LINE

Once per session, when you choose to protect someone or something through direct confrontation, you can declare:

**"I hold the line."**

You cannot be moved, bypassed, or taken out until:
- The thing you're protecting is safe, OR
- You choose to fall back, OR
- You're completely overwhelmed (GM's call, requires serious force)

While holding the line:
- You take +1 to all defensive actions
- You cannot leave your position
- Enemies must go through you or find another way
- You can Take the Hit for anyone behind you without rolling

When you finally fall (if you do), it's cinematic. You take everyone nearby with you. Describe how you go down fighting.

## QUESTIONS TO ANSWER (Discover through play)

You don't need answers now. Find them during the game:

1. **What job made you realize violence wasn't the solution anymore?** (Or did you realize that at all?)
2. **Who did you hurt that you still think about?** What would you say to them?
3. **What would you do if your employer ordered you to kill someone from the crew?** Would you follow orders?
4. **What's the difference between you and the corporate security who beat down Golgotha miners?** Is there a difference?
5. **When did you last feel afraid?** What scared you?
6. **What would you do if you didn't do this?** Can you even imagine another life?
7. **Who taught you to be this good at violence?** Do you still talk to them?

## EXAMPLE: MARCUS "SLAB" VENN

**High Concept**: *Former Sidereal Station Security, Now Freelance Blade*  
**Trouble**: *I Follow Orders, Even the Bad Ones*  
**Connection**: *My Daughter Lives in Sidereal's Residential Ring—I Can't Go Back*

**Stats**: Body +3, Reflexes +2, Code +0, Tech +1, Cool +2

**Chrome**:
- Subdermal Armor Plating (+2 Meat, -1 Nerves)
- Tactical Combat HUD (+1 Systems, -1 Meat)

**Final Stress**: Meat ▢▢▢▢▢ | Nerves ▢▢ | Systems ▢▢▢▢

**Talents**:
- Combat Veteran (4 points)
- Read the Room (2 points)

**Gear**: Sidereal Arms heavy pistol, combat knife, tactical vest, secure comm, restraints, 2,000 credits

**Notes**: Slab worked station security for twelve years. Professional, efficient, loyal. Then Meridian Holdings ordered him to disappear an engineer who found something in the zero-g labs. He did it. It cost him everything—his job, his family, his ability to look his daughter in the face. Now he works freelance, takes jobs in the system, and sends anonymous money to his daughter's account. She probably knows. He can never go back to Sidereal. That doesn't mean he's reformed. He just picks his orders more carefully now. Every job he takes, he assesses: Is this the kind of order that would make him disappear someone again? So far, the answer's been no. But he's not sure what he'll do if the money's good enough and someone desperate enough asks.

You don't ask if violence is necessary—you are violence, controlled and professional, and you decide when to deploy it.
